## Why This Exists

The AI industry is centralizing power faster than we can regulate it. Dense transformers require $10M+ training runs. Neuromorphic hardware is gatekept by ~200 labs worldwide. Proprietary models are locked behind APIs.

The *Drosophila* connectome was published in Nature 2024 — 139,000 neurons, 50 million synapses, CC BY 4.0. A gift to science. But the tooling to run it, perturb it, and learn from it is scattered, underfunded, and GPU-gated.

**I am building the compiler that bridges this gap. Alone, for now, with $150k and a working prototype.**

## The Go-to-Market: Bottom-Up

I am not building a sales team. I am publishing a working connectome simulator to the public web. When a neuroscientist at Stanford hits the rate limit on the free sandbox and emails me asking for more, that is the sales motion — and it costs zero dollars in CAC.

**The three channels, in priority order:**

1. **Free sandbox** — Published URL. Anyone can run 130k neurons in their browser. 100 activations/day free. Opt-in telemetry seeds the data moat.
2. **STTR grants** — Partner with university labs on federal grant applications. Non-dilutive funding that validates the science and builds academic relationships simultaneously.
3. **Enterprise inbound** — When a lab or biotech outgrows the sandbox and requests dedicated infrastructure. Priced per deal, not per tier.

---

## The STTR Pipeline

STTR grants are the primary initial revenue channel for a solo operator. A solo founder cannot cold-call biotech procurement. But a solo founder can partner with a PI at Stanford on an NSF application.

**Target pipeline:**
- 3 Letters of Intent from university labs (Stanford, MIT, UCSD)
- Phase I submissions by Month 4 post-funding (~$250k each)
- Phase II follow-on at Month 12-18 (~$1M each)

**Current reality:** No LOIs secured. Conversations active. The sandbox is the conversion tool.
