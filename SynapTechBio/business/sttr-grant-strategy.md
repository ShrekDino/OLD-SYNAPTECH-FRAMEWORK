# STTR Grant Strategy (Corrected — Primary Channel)

> *STTR grants are the primary initial revenue channel for a solo operator.*

## Rationale

A solo founder cannot cold-call biotech procurement. But a solo founder can email a PI at Stanford and say "I built a connectome engine. Want to co-author an STTR application?"

| Channel | Timeline | Cost | Solo-Achievable |
|---------|----------|------|-----------------|
| Institutional sales | 9-12 months | $50k CAC | ❌ |
| VC round | 6-12 months | 15-25% dilution | ❌ (needs traction) |
| STTR Phase I | 3-9 months | $0 (non-dilutive) | ✅ |
| Angel investors | 3-6 months | 10-20% dilution | ⚠️ |

## LOI Strategy

1. Deploy sandbox → get a working URL
2. Cold-email 20 PIs at target universities
3. Offer co-authorship on any publication using the platform
4. Secure non-binding LOIs expressing intent to collaborate on STTR applications
5. Present LOIs to investors as pipeline validation

**Current reality:** No LOIs secured. Conversations require the sandbox to convert.
