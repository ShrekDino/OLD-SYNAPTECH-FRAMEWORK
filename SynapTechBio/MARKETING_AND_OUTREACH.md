# SynapTechBio — Marketing & Outreach Strategy

> *Building in the open. Growing through genuine connection. Making the neuromorphic commons a reality, together.*
>
> **Current reality:** No funding. No hardware. One founder. Every connection matters.

---

## 1. Our Philosophy: Building in the Open

SynapTechBio's marketing is not about broadcasting — it's about **building in public** and letting the work speak for itself. Every commit, every breakthrough, every lesson learned is an opportunity to connect with the people who will shape the future of neural computation.

**The core principle:** Share the journey authentically. When people see real progress, real science, and real passion, they naturally want to be part of it.

## 2. LinkedIn Strategy

LinkedIn is our primary platform for community growth and connection-building. The approach is organic, relationship-driven, and multi-audience.

### 2.1 Content Framework

| Content Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Progress updates** | 2-3×/week | Share commits, milestones, breakthroughs |
| **Technical deep-dives** | 1×/week | Explain IDRE architecture, LSM mechanics, CSC math |
| **Science spotlights** | 1×/week | Highlight connectomics research, fly brain insights |
| **Philosophical threads** | 1×/2 weeks | Transhumanist rights, open science, democratized AI |
| **Connection spotlights** | 1×/week | Feature collaborators, researchers, community members |
| **Personal narrative** | 1×/week | The founder's journey — building solo, lessons learned |

### 2.2 Audiences & Connection Strategy

#### Neuroscientists & Neurobiology Students
- **Why they matter**: Domain experts who validate the science, potential research partners, STTR collaborators
- **What to share**: Connectome visualization demos, LSM performance benchmarks, neuro-somatic proxy research
- **How to connect**: Comment on their published research, ask genuine questions about their work, share how IDRE relates to their findings
- **Connection hooks**: "Your work on [specific study] directly relates to how we're approaching [IDRE feature]. Would love to hear your thoughts."

#### Computer Science & AI/ML Engineers
- **Why they matter**: Builders who can contribute code, adopt RPaaS, push the technology forward
- **What to share**: Sparse matrix benchmarks, CuPy vs SciPy tradeoffs, SSE streaming architecture, 3D visualization techniques
- **How to connect**: Engage on their posts about ML infrastructure, CUDA optimization, real-time systems
- **Connection hooks**: "We solved [specific engineering problem] using [approach]. Curious how you'd tackle [remaining challenge]."

#### IT Infrastructure & DevOps
- **Why they matter**: Infrastructure partners for cloud Loihi deployment, Docker/K8s expertise, reliability engineering
- **What to share**: Deployment architectures, GPU cluster management, monitoring/observability patterns
- **How to connect**: Engage on posts about Docker, Lambda Labs, GPU provisioning, Prometheus monitoring

#### Cybersecurity Professionals
- **Why they matter**: DCSL requires serious security review, penetration testing, cryptographic audit
- **What to share**: AES-256-GCM implementation details, DCSL architecture, threat model discussions
- **How to connect**: Ask for feedback on threat models, share encryption architecture, invite security audits
- **Connection hooks**: "We're building a cryptographic IP protection layer for neuroscience research. Would value your perspective on our threat model."

#### BioTech & NeuroTech Professionals
- **Why they matter**: Potential enterprise customers, clinical research partners, industry validation
- **What to share**: RPaaS case studies, clinical applications, regulatory pathway thinking
- **How to connect**: Engage on neurotech industry content, share insights on neurotechnology market trends

#### Open Source Community
- **Why they matter**: Contributors, advocates, the people who make the commons real
- **What to share**: CONTRIBUTING.md updates, good-first-issue tags, contributor spotlights
- **How to connect**: Be responsive, grateful, and transparent. Recognize every contribution.

#### Academic Researchers & Professors
- **Why they matter**: STTR partners, grant collaborators, lab adoption
- **What to share**: Research results, benchmark data, publication-ready metrics
- **How to connect**: Attend virtual conferences, engage with their lab's published work, propose joint grant applications

#### Students (Undergrad & Graduate)
- **Why they matter**: Future neuroscientists, engineers, and community members
- **What to share**: Learning resources, connectome visualizations, internship/research opportunities
- **How to connect**: Offer mentorship, answer questions, create beginner-friendly content

#### Investors & Venture Community
- **Why they matter**: Future funding, strategic guidance, network access
- **What to share**: Milestone announcements, metrics, competitive positioning
- **How to connect**: Warm introductions through existing connections, YC/NEA demo events

## 3. Connection Flow

```
1. Follow → Engage meaningfully on their content
2. DM → Specific, personalized message referencing their work
3. Share → Show them how SynapTechBio connects to their interests
4. Collaborate → Offer something of value (early access, data, discussion)
5. Amplify → Share their work, give credit, build relationship
```

### DM Template Framework

```
Hi [Name],

I came across your work on [specific topic/project] and found it really compelling.
Specifically, [specific detail that shows you actually read/engaged].

I'm building SynapTechBio — we're [1-sentence description].
One thing we're working on that connects to your work: [specific connection].

Would love to hear your perspective if you have a moment.

Best,
Sami
```

## 7. Collaboration Infrastructure

The repo itself is a collaboration tool:

- **Issues** tagged `good-first-issue` for new contributors
- **Issues** tagged `research-proposal` for scientific collaboration
- **Issues** tagged `collaboration-offer` for partnership discussions
- **Open RFC process** for community governance decisions
- **GitHub Discussions** for open Q&A and brainstorming

## 8. Tracking & Iteration

Keep a simple log of:
- Connections made per week (by audience category)
- Conversations that led to meaningful engagement
- Content that resonated most (engagement metrics)
- Collaboration opportunities that emerged

Review monthly and iterate on what's working.
