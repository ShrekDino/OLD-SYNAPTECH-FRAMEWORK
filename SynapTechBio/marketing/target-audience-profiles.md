# SynapTechBio — Target Audience Profiles

> *Detailed audience breakdowns for connection-building and community growth.*

---

## Neuroscientists

**Demographics**: PhDs, postdocs, research scientists in academia and industry
**Count**: ~50,000 globally (Society for Neuroscience membership)
**Pain Points**: Connectome data is scattered, tools are underfunded, Loihi access is grant-gated
**Value Prop**: Free cloud Loihi access + IDRE platform. Keep your IP. Contribute to the Living Blueprint.
**Connection Approach**: Engage with published research. Share how IDRE connects to their specific domain.
**Content That Resonates**: Connectome visualizations, spMV benchmarks, neuro-somatic proxy research

## Neurobiology Students (Undergrad & Graduate)

**Demographics**: BS/MS/PhD students in neuroscience, computational neuroscience
**Count**: ~15,000 active in connectomics-related programs
**Pain Points**: Expensive compute access, lack of hands-on neuromorphic experience
**Value Prop**: Free tier platform access, learning resources, research project opportunities
**Connection Approach**: Offer mentorship, answer questions, share educational content
**Content That Resonates**: How-to guides, architecture explanations, career insights

## Computer Science / AI Engineers

**Demographics**: Software engineers, ML engineers, AI researchers (industry and academic)
**Count**: Millions globally
**Pain Points**: Dense transformers hitting scaling walls, interest in alternative architectures
**Value Prop**: Real bio-inspired neural computation, sparse graph algorithms, neuromorphic hardware access
**Connection Approach**: Share technical deep-dives on CSC engine, CuPy optimization, SSE streaming
**Content That Resonates**: Code snippets, architecture diagrams, performance benchmarks, git commits

## IT Infrastructure / DevOps

**Demographics**: Platform engineers, SREs, cloud architects
**Count**: ~5M globally
**Pain Points**: GPU cluster management, CI/CD for ML pipelines
**Value Prop**: Docker Compose deployment, Lambda Labs integration, Prometheus monitoring
**Connection Approach**: Share deployment configurations, Dockerfiles, observability patterns
**Content That Resonates**: docker-compose.yml examples, CI workflows, monitoring dashboards

## Cybersecurity Professionals

**Demographics**: Security engineers, cryptographers, penetration testers
**Count**: ~4M globally (ISC²)
**Pain Points**: Emerging threat models for neural data, cryptographic IP protection
**Value Prop**: DCSL architecture to audit, AES-256-GCM implementation to review, threat model discussions
**Connection Approach**: Ask for security feedback, share threat models, invite audits
**Content That Resonates**: Encryption architecture, DCSL design documents, threat model discussions

## BioTech / NeuroTech Industry

**Demographics**: Executives, R&D directors, product managers at neurotech companies
**Count**: ~50k in neurotech industry (Neurotech Leaders Report)
**Pain Points**: Slow R&D cycles, expensive compute, regulatory uncertainty
**Value Prop**: RPaaS for faster research, DCSL for IP protection, STTR for non-dilutive funding
**Connection Approach**: Share industry insights, market analysis, regulatory pathway thinking
**Content That Resonates**: Use cases, case studies, market data, competitive analysis

## Open Source Contributors

**Demographics**: Developers who contribute to open source projects
**Count**: ~28M GitHub users
**Pain Points**: Looking for meaningful projects with real scientific impact
**Value Prop**: MIT-licensed codebase, good-first-issue tags, real science at stake
**Connection Approach**: Make contributing easy, recognize contributions publicly, maintain active issues
**Content That Resonates**: CONTRIBUTING.md, good-first-issue tags, contributor spotlights

## Academic Researchers / Professors

**Demographics**: PIs, lab directors, department chairs
**Count**: ~10k in connectomics/neuroscience
**Pain Points**: Grant writing burden, compute costs for students, reproducibility challenges
**Value Prop**: STTR partnership framework, free platform access for labs, published benchmarks
**Connection Approach**: Cite their work, propose collaborations, attend conferences
**Content That Resonates**: Benchmark results, publication drafts, partnership proposals

## Students (All Disciplines)

**Demographics**: Undergrad and graduate students in STEM
**Count**: Millions globally
**Pain Points**: Need research experience, portfolio projects, mentorship
**Value Prop**: Real research platform to learn on, open source contributions, community mentorship
**Connection Approach**: Offer help, create learning resources, maintain beginner-friendly issues
**Content That Resonates**: Tutorials, getting-started guides, project ideas, internship announcements

## Investors / VCs

**Demographics**: Angel investors, seed funds, deep tech VCs
**Count**: ~10k active in AI/deep tech
**Pain Points**: Finding capital-efficient, defensible deep tech with real science
**Value Prop**: $150k for 26 weeks of milestones, DCSL moat, $1.8T TAM, STTR leverage
**Connection Approach**: Warm introductions, demo events, milestone announcements
**Content That Resonates**: Milestone updates, KPIs, competitive positioning, market analysis

## How to Prioritize

1. **Phase 1 (Now)**: Neuroscientists + CS/AI Engineers + Students — building the core community
2. **Phase 2 (Seed)**: BioTech + Academic Researchers + Open Source — expanding adoption
3. **Phase 3 (Series A)**: IT/DevOps + Cybersecurity + Investors — scaling infrastructure

> *Every connection is a potential collaborator. Every conversation is a chance to learn. Build in public, build with people, and let the community grow organically.*
