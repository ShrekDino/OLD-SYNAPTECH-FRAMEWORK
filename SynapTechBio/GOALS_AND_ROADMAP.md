## Research Track: Consciousness Validation Suite

Alongside the engineering roadmap, the [Consciousness Simulation Diagnostic Framework (CSDF)](https://github.com/ShrekDino/uploaded-consciousness-framework) provides a formal specification and validation layer for synthetic consciousness architectures. The CSDF enumerates failure modes in:

- **Thermodynamic viability** — Szilard engine bounds, Landauer floor, DQFR duty cycling
- **Topological consensus** — GWFR unbalanced optimal transport, $\Omega_{\text{coherence}}$ coherence radius
- **Relational protocols** — cross-node mutual information, recursive senescence prevention
- **Temporal traversal** — adiabatic windowing, relativistic gauge anchoring, $\mathcal{V}_T$ velocity control
- **Language acquisition** — active inference over token sequences, entropy collapse monitoring

The CSDF is the formal specification against which the IDRE connectome engine and FlyWire LSM are validated. Each failure mode documented in the CSDF registry constitutes a constraint on the hypothesis space for the next architectural iteration.
