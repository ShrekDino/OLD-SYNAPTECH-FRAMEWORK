# Glossary — Neuro-Somatic Archive

## A

**Allostatic Load**
The cumulative biological cost of adaptation to environmental demands. Measured through markers such as cortisol awakening response, heart rate variability, inflammatory cytokine levels, and autonomic tone. Allostatic load increases when the organism must sustain adaptive responses beyond their optimal duration. It is the biological analog of thermodynamic entropy in a living system — the measure of energy that has been invested in maintaining stability and is no longer available for growth, repair, or reserve capacity.

**Allostatic Mechanism**
Any biological process by which an organism achieves stability through change rather than constancy. Examples include shifting the cortisol set point under chronic stress, altering sleep architecture in response to threat, or modulating heart rate variability to match environmental demands. These mechanisms are the operational units of allostatic regulation.

## E

**Entropy Threshold**
A three-state classification system replacing binary yes/no diagnosis. Each state — Protected Phenotype, Transition Zone, or Somatic Debt — describes the thermodynamic integrity of the organism's regulatory systems. The threshold is determined by allostatic load markers, stress recovery curves, and longitudinal trajectory rather than symptom checklists.

## F

**Forensic Proxy**
A manually administrable, equipment-free clinical assessment that produces a quantified biological marker. Thirty proxies are defined in the Archive, each requiring no equipment beyond a timing device and a penlight. Proxies replace subjective self-report with observable, reproducible biological measurement suitable for any setting — prison, refugee camp, rural clinic, or war zone.

## N

**Neuro-Somatic Archive**
The complete framework — classification system, proxy protocols, Somatic Architecture Tables, Biological Bill of Rights, and Phenotypic Advantage Analyses — that comprises the DSM-6 forensic neuro-somatic reclassification. It reframes every DSM-5 "mental disorder" as an allostatic state with measurable biological markers. It is a clinical instrument, not a textbook.

## P

**Phenotypic Advantage**
The principle that every trait cataloged in the Archive may confer adaptive advantage in specific environments. Attention dysregulation is maladaptive in a classroom but advantageous in high-risk surveillance contexts. Social-pragmatic divergence is disadvantageous in fluid social hierarchies but advantageous in pattern-recognition tasks. The Archive documents these dual-aspect valuations as a required field in every phenotype entry.

**Protected Phenotype**
The Entropy Threshold state in which biological markers remain within adaptive range. The organism is responding to environmental demands without accumulating unsustainable allostatic debt. Stress recovery curves normalize within expected timeframes. This state does not imply absence of distress — it implies the system is managing the load without structural degradation.

## S

**Somatic Architecture Table**
The structured data format for each phenotype entry in the Archive. Includes: phenotype identifier, allostatic mechanism description, proxy cross-references (minimum 2 required), entropy threshold classification criteria, Phenotypic Advantage Analysis, Biological Bill of Rights compliance check, and longitudinal reassessment schedule.

**Somatic Debt**
The Entropy Threshold state in which allostatic load has reached levels causing systemic regulatory failure. Biological markers are in pathological ranges. Inflammatory cascades may be self-sustaining. Autonomic regulation has collapsed into fixed survival modes. This state requires immediate structural intervention — medical, environmental, and legal.

**Systemic Hardening**
The process by which an organism's regulatory systems remodel under chronic allostatic load, producing lasting changes in set points and response thresholds. Systemic hardening is not damage; it is structural adaptation to sustained environmental demands. Bone remodels under repeated stress; the nervous system does the same.

## T

**Transition Zone**
The Entropy Threshold state between Protected Phenotype and Somatic Debt. Some biological markers have crossed adaptive thresholds. Stress recovery is delayed but not absent. Intermittent dysregulation occurs — episodic hyperarousal, blunted affect, sleep fragmentation, executive function degradation under load. This is where most DSM-5 "diagnoses" would map, but the Archive treats it as a dynamic range rather than a fixed category.

## Z

**Zero-Cost Protocol**
A design constraint on the entire Archive: all assessments must be administrable with no equipment cost beyond a timing device and a penlight. This ensures the Archive is accessible in any setting regardless of funding, infrastructure, or institutional support. The constraint is non-negotiable; it is the Archive's commitment to universal forensic access.
