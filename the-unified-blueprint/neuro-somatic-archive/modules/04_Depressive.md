# Module 04 — Depressive Phenotypes

Depressive phenotypes are framed as glucocorticoid-mediated energy conservation states with demonstrable HPA axis dysregulation, inflammatory activation, and reward-circuit hypofunction. Each entry specifies the biological origins of depressive symptomatology.

### Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Phenotype (formerly Disruptive

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A developmental allostatic state characterized by chronic, non-episodic irritability rooted in immature prefrontal inhibitory gating of amygdala–thalamic threat circuits. The prefrontal cortex has not yet developed the structural connectivity (uncinate fasciculus myelination) required for topdown regulation of subcortical arousal. The temper outbursts represent a low-threshold amygdala– hypothalamic–brainstem escape — a hardwired defense program released by insufficient prefrontal braking. Unlike bipolar phenotypes, the irritability is tonic, not phasic, and does not follow an episodic oscillator pattern.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to developmental factoring: every outburst must be weight-adjusted against the child’s uncinate maturation, not adult prefrontal norms.
2. Right to setting-specific induction: failure in one setting (home) does not constitute a trait when the other setting (school) is intact.
3. Right to state separation: the outburst is an involuntary brainstem defense program, not volitional oppositionality.
4. Right to neuroimaging protection: no chronic medication for mood dysregulation without DTI confirmation of uncinate FA relative to age norms.

### Major Depressive Phenotype (formerly Major Depressive Disorder

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A glucocorticoid-mediated energy conservation state with demonstrable HPA axis non-suppression. CRH hypersecretion from the paraventricular nucleus produces adrenal hypercortisolemia that downregulates hippocampal glucocorticoid receptors, impairing negative feedback. Tryptophan– kynurenine pathway shunting reduces central serotonin synthesis. Pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α) activate insular and anterior cingulate sickness-behavior circuits. Striatal dopamine D1 receptor downregulation produces anhedonia. The phenotype is defined by duration (≥ 2 weeks) and symptom count (≥ 5 of 9), not by chronicity per se.

### Persistent Depressive Phenotype (formerly Persistent Depressive

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A low-grade, chronic glucocorticoid adaptation sustained for ≥ 2 years (≥ 1 year pediatric). The HPA axis is tonically elevated but has not crossed into the hypercortisolemic escape seen in acute Major Depressive episodes. The system has adapted to the elevated allostatic load by resetting its set point: what is reported as “baseline” mood is actually a depressed steady state. Prefrontal cortical volume is reduced (chronic dendritic retraction) and the default-mode network is tonically hyperconnected. The key distinction from Major Depressive Phenotype is temporal: the state is traitlike, not episodic.

### Premenstrual Dysphoric Phenotype (formerly Premenstrual

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A cyclic neuroendocrine sensitivity state driven by normal luteal-phase progesterone elevation in individuals with an abnormal central response to allopregnanolone, a GABA-A receptor–modulating progesterone metabolite. In affected individuals, allopregnanolone fails to potentiate GABA-A currents normally and instead produces paradoxical anxiogenic effects, possibly via subunit composition differences (reduced δ subunit expression). The result is a luteal-phase window of GABAergic dysregulation characterized by subcortical disinhibition. The phenotype is defined by its strict temporal coupling to the menstrual cycle: symptom onset in the late luteal phase, remission within days of menses, and absence in the follicular phase.

### Substance/Medication-Induced Depressive Phenotype (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

An exogenously triggered depressive state with a defined pharmacological initiator. The depressive collapse is driven by drug-induced depletion of monoamines (reserpine, methyldopa), cytokine induction (interferon-alpha), or direct neurotoxicity (alcohol withdrawal). The key forensic feature is the temporal coupling between substance exposure and depressive onset, and the expected resolution upon substance elimination. The depressive state tracks drug level, elimination half-life, and withdrawal kinetics. Unlike the primary Major Depressive Phenotype, this form is pharmacodynamically determined and will self-correct if the trigger is removed — unless it kindles an independent depressive oscillator.

### Medical Condition–Coupled Depressive Phenotype (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A depressive presentation arising directly from the pathophysiological effects of a medical condition — endocrine (Cushing’s, hypothyroidism), neurological (Parkinson’s, Huntington’s, stroke, TBI), inflammatory (SLE, MS), or neoplastic. The depressive state is the direct neurobiological consequence of medical pathophysiology: inflammatory cytokines crossing the blood–brain barrier, basal ganglia dopamine depletion (Parkinson’s), caudate atrophy (Huntington’s), or structural disconnection (stroke). Treatment of the underlying condition typically ameliorates the depressive state.

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A collection of incomplete depressive presentations that do not meet full duration or symptom thresholds: recurrent brief depression (2–13 day episodes, monthly for 12 months), short-duration depressive episode (4–13 days), or depressive episode with insufficient symptoms (< 5 of 9). These represent abortive depressive events where the allostatic load is sufficient to produce partial activation of the depressive cascade but insufficient to sustain the full 14-day, 5-symptom phenotype. ### Unspecified Depressive Phenotype (formerly Unspecified

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A placeholder category for presentations with insufficient biometric data to assign a specific depressive phenotype. No biological state is asserted; the designation is a data-gap marker. Once sufficient longitudinal autonomic data (≥ 14 days of actigraphy + HRV + daily mood sampling) are acquired, the presentation migrates to an above phenotype.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to data sufficiency: no phenotype assigned without ≥ 14 days of continuous actigraphy, HRV, and daily mood sampling.
2. Right to provisional holding: the unspecified designation is a temporary data gap, not a diagnosis.
3. Right to retrospective data inclusion: previously recorded biometric data (fitness trackers, phone sensors) are admissible for phenotype assignment.
