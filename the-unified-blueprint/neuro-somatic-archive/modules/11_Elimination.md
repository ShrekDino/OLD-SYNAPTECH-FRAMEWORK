# Module 11 — Elimination Phenotypes

This module covers disruptions in urinary and fecal elimination circuits, reclassified as neuro-somatic dysregulation of bladder-sphincter and colonic-anorectal coordination. Phenotypes range from developmental delays in continence maturation to functional elimination disorders, with emphasis on objective physiological markers.

### 11.1 Enuresis Phenotype (formerly Enuresis 307.6 [F98.0])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Bladder-sphincter coordination dysmaturity with delayed or disrupted development of circadian vasopressin rhythm, reduced functional bladder capacity, and/or detrusor hyperreactivity. The organism has not established or has lost the integrated neuro-muscular reflex of conscious urinary retention and coordinated voiding only in socially appropriate contexts.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A. Repeated voiding of urine into bed or clothes, involuntary or intentional | Bladder-sphincter coordination failure; absent or disrupted conscious voiding inhibition | Detrusor hyperreactivity; reduced functional bladder capacity; nocturnal polyuria |
| B. ≥2x/week for 3 months or clinically significant distress | Established pattern of elimination dysregulation | Automatic voiding reflex triggers without cortical override |
| C. Chronological age ≥5 years (or equivalent developmental level) | Developmental window for continence maturation has passed | Neurodevelopmental delay in pontine micturition center–prefrontal circuit maturation |
| D. Not attributable to substance or medical condition | Primary developmental/functional elimination; not secondary to organic pathology | Normal urinalysis; normal renal ultrasound; no diabetes or neurological disease |
| Nocturnal only | Voiding during sleep; absent arousal to bladder fullness | Delayed maturation of nocturnal vasopressin rhythm; reduced functional bladder capacity sleep |
| Diurnal only | Voiding during waking hours; often conscious deferral until overflow | Detrusor instability (urge type) or urge incontinence (postponement type) |
| Nocturnal and diurnal | Combined subtype; most severe | Multiple contributory mechanisms; developmental delay |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Wet-night diary | Caregiver-computed record of nocturnal enuresis events over 14 consecutive nights | ≥4 wet nights per 14-day period |
| First morning urine osmolality | Urine specific gravity or osmolality on first morning void | <800 mOsm/kg (inadequate nocturnal vasopressin) |
| Functional bladder capacity | Maximum voided volume on 48-hour frequency-volume chart | <70% of expected capacity for age (age [years] × 30 + 30 mL) |
| Post-void residual bladder volume | Bladder ultrasound within 5 minutes of voiding | >20 mL (incomplete emptying suggesting dysfunctional voiding) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | <2 wet nights/week; no daytime wetting; age-appropriate bladder capacity | Bladder capacity >85% expected; urine osmolality >800; normal voiding pattern |
| Transition Zone | 2–4 wet nights/week; occasional daytime accidents | Bladder capacity 70%–85% expected; osmolality 600–800; mild detrusor instability |
| Somatic Debt | >4 wet nights/week; daily daytime wetting; post-void residual >20 mL | Bladder capacity <70%; osmolality <600; detrusor hyperreactivity on urodynamics |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to differentiation from organic pathology (UTI, diabetes, neurogenic bladder) before behavioral classification.
2. The right to interventions targeting the specific biological mechanism: desmopressin for nocturnal polyuria, anticholinergics for detrusor hyperreactivity, alarm therapy for arousal deficit.
3. The right to protection from punishment for involuntary voiding, which is neurodevelopmentally determined.
4. The right to bladder capacity training and pelvic floor physiotherapy for functional capacity deficits.

### 11.2 Encopresis Phenotype (formerly Encopresis 307.7 [F98.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Colonic-anorectal evacuation dysregulation with stool retention, overflow incontinence, or intentional inappropriate defecation. The organism either retains stool leading to constipation with overflow leakage (retentive subtype) or deposits feces in inappropriate contexts without constipation (non-retentive subtype), reflecting disrupted enteric-brain axis signaling.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A. Repeated passage of feces into inappropriate places, involuntary or intentional | Evacuation reflex dysregulation; absent or suppressed conscious defecation control | Altered rectal sensation threshold; dyssynergic defecation; or intentional oppositional behavior |
| B. ≥1 event/month for 3 months | Established pattern of inappropriate fecal elimination | Chronic rectal distention; desensitized stretch receptors (retentive); or conditioned behavioral pattern (non-retentive) |
| C. Chronological age ≥4 years (or equivalent developmental level) | Developmental window for bowel continence maturation has passed | Neurodevelopmental delay in anosphincteric-prefrontal circuit |
| D. Not attributable to substance or medical condition | Primary functional elimination; not Hirschsprung disease, spina bifida, or other structural pathology | Normal anorectal manometry; normal rectal biopsy in retentive subtype |
| With constipation and overflow incontinence | Fecal retention with secondary leakage; stool often poorly formed | Abdominal radiograph showing retained stool; dilated rectum; desensitized rectal stretch receptors |
| Without constipation and overflow incontinence | Normal stool consistency; intermittent soiling; often oppositional | Normal rectal diameter; no stool retention; possible conduct disorder features |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Abdominal radiograph (KUB) | Plain film: retained stool assessment using Barr or Leech scoring system | Barr score >10 (moderate to severe fecal loading) |
| Digital rectal examination | Palpation of rectal vault for stool mass | Hard or impacted stool in rectal ampulla; dilated rectal vault |
| Soiling frequency diary | Caregiver record of all fecal soiling events over 4 weeks | ≥4 soiling events per month |
| Anorectal manometry | Measure resting and squeeze anal sphincter pressures; rectal sensation threshold | Abnormal rectal sensation threshold (>30 mL for first sensation); dyssynergic defecation pattern |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | <1 soiling event/month; no constipation; normal stool pattern | Normal abdominal radiograph; normal rectal sensation |
| Transition Zone | 1–4 soiling events/month; mild constipation; occasional withholding | Mild-moderate fecal loading on radiograph; elevated rectal sensation threshold |
| Somatic Debt | >4 soiling events/month; severe constipation; overflow incontinence daily | Severe fecal loading (Barr >15); dilated rectum; absent rectal sensation; dyssynergic defecation |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to abdominal radiograph or other objective assessment of fecal loading before diagnosis.
2. The right to aggressive bowel cleanout and maintenance laxative therapy for retentive subtype before behavioral intervention.
3. The right to anorectal manometry to identify paradoxical sphincter contraction (dyssynergic defecation) for targeted biofeedback.
4. The right to protection from punishment for involuntary soiling, which is a neurophysiological evacuation failure, not willful opposition.

### 11.3 Other Specified Elimination Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Elimination Disorder 788.39 [N39.498] / 787.60 [R15.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Subthreshold elimination dysregulation that does not fully meet enuresis or encopresis criteria but produces clinically significant distress or impairment.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| Subtype | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Low-frequency enuresis | Bladder-sphincter dysmaturity at insufficient frequency for full criteria | Detrusor instability <2x/week or <3 months duration |
| Other urinary symptoms | Urinary elimination dysregulation not meeting enuresis criteria | Daytime frequency syndrome; dysfunctional voiding; giggle incontinence |
| Low-frequency encopresis | Evacuation dysregulation at <1 event/month | Mild fecal retention; intermittent soiling |
| Other fecal symptoms | Fecal elimination dysregulation not meeting encopresis criteria | Functional constipation without soiling; stool-withholding behavior |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

Use subtype-matched proxy protocols from the corresponding full phenotype above.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to clinical intervention proportional to symptom burden regardless of event frequency.
2. The right to monitoring for progression to full phenotype.
3. The right to the same organic disease exclusion workup as full phenotypes.
4. The right to reclassification when frequency or duration criteria are met.

### 11.4 Unspecified Elimination Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Elimination Disorder 788.30 [R32] / 787.60 [R15.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Uncharacterized elimination dysregulation with insufficient information for subtype specification.

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

Use domain-appropriate assessment: wet-night diary, soiling frequency diary, abdominal radiograph, urinalysis. Reassess when more information is available.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to provisional classification without premature commitment to a specific elimination circuit.
2. The right to organic pathology exclusion before behavioral classification.
3. The right to symptom management during diagnostic clarification.
4. The right to reclassification when adequate biological data are obtained.
