# Module 07 — Trauma- and Stressor-Related Adaptations

Phenotypes from the Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6), reclassifying DSM-5 Trauma- and Stressor-Related Adaptations disorders through a neuro-somatic lens.

### Phenotypes Attachment Inhibition Phenotype (formerly Reactive Attachment Disorder 313.89 [F94.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Primary attachment circuitry suppression via medial prefrontal cortex inhibition of oxytocinergic and dopaminergic reward pathways during caregiver interaction. Chronic hypercortisolism secondary to inadequate caregiving produces downregulation of ventral striatal social reward processing and blunted autonomic response to relational cues.

### Disinhibited Social Approach Phenotype (formerly Disinhibited Social Engagement Disorder 313.89 [F94.2])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Failure of amygdala-based threat discrimination circuitry due to insufficient peripubertal pruning of indiscriminate approach behaviors. Orbitofrontal cortex hypoactivation fails to generate socially appropriate avoidance signals. Ventral striatum remains hyperresponsive to novel social stimuli, overriding the typical fear-based gating mechanism.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Reduced reticence with unfamiliar adults | Amygdala fails to generate typical threat- potentiated startle to strangers | Amygdalar hyporeactivity to novel conspecifics |
| A2: Overly familiar verbal/ physical behavior | Orbitofrontal cortex does not register social boundary violations | OFC hypoactivation during social approach |
| A3: Diminished checking back with caregiver | Hippocampal contextual threat memory insufficiently recruits prefrontal safety signaling | Reduced hippocampal-prefrontal theta coupling |
| A4: Willingness to go with unfamiliar adult | Oxytocinergic attachment system fails to gate approach/avoidance | Aberrant oxytocin receptor distribution |
| B: Not limited to impulsivity | Specific failure in social fear learning, not generalized motor impulsivity | Dissociable from frontostriatal ADHD circuits |
| C: Extremes of insufficient care | Developmental absence of selective attachment template | Impaired cortical mapping of familiar vs. unfamiliar faces |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Skin Conductance Response to Stranger | Measure SCR during approach by unfamiliar adult vs. caregiver | SCR <0.5 µS differential between stranger and caregiver |
| Startle Response Magnitude | Electromyographic eyeblink to 95 dB white noise during stranger approach | <40% of age-normative startle potentiation |
| Pupillary Discrimination Index | Pupil diameter change to caregiver face vs. stranger face | <1 mm difference between conditions |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Age-appropriate stranger reticence; SCR discriminates familiar/unfamiliar | SCR differential >1.0 µS; startle potentiation >60% |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The Right to amygdala-based threat discrimination that gates social approach behavior
2. The Right to orbitofrontal registration of social boundary violations
3. The Right to selective attachment to familiar caregivers as a biological template for social safety
4. The Right to species-typical development of the fear-potentiated startle circuit.

### Posttraumatic Stress Phenotype (formerly Posttraumatic Stress Disorder 309.81 [F43.10])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Permanent sensitization of the amygdala-central nucleus-locus coeruleus threat detection circuit following trauma encoding. Hippocampal volume reduction impairs contextual discrimination, producing generalized threat generalization. Medial prefrontal cortex fails to exert inhibitory topdown control over amygdala, producing chronic hyperarousal. Dissociative subtype involves additional dorsolateral PFC suppression of limbic output, producing emotional numbing via prefrontal-limbic decoupling.

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Hippocampal-prefrontal memory consolidation disrupted by excessive glucocorticoid signaling, producing fragmented trauma encoding and peritraumatic dissociation. ### Adjustment Phenotypes (formerly Adjustment Disorders 309.x [F43.2x])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Subcortical stress-response circuitry activation proportional to a psychosocial stressor that does not meet trauma threshold. HPA axis elevation produces downstream effects on mood, anxiety, or conduct regulation circuits. Unlike PTSD, no permanent threat-circuit sensitization occurs; symptom expression maps to the specific circuit most vulnerable in the individual’s neuro-somatic architecture.

### Other Specified Trauma/Stressor Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorder 309.89 [F43.8])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Partial threat-sensitization or atypical stress-response circuit activation that does not meet full criteria of any specified trauma phenotype but produces quantifiable allostatic load. Includes: adjustment-like reactions with delayed onset (>3 months); prolonged duration (>6 months without sustained stressor); cultural syndromes (ataque de nervios); persistent complex bereavement.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms predominate but full criteria not met | Subthreshold threat-circuit sensitization | Partial amygdala-PFC dysregulation |
| Clinician specifies cultural syndrome | Culturally shaped circuit activation | Context-specific HPA/autonomic |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Heart Rate Reactivity RSA | 3-min stressor narrative vs. neutral 5-min resting baseline | HR increase >10 bpm but below full PTSD threshold RSA 1.2-1.8 ms² (subthreshold range) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Transition Zone | Normal stress response Partial symptom picture | All biomarkers within normative range Subthreshold deviations on 1-2 biomarkers |
| Somatic Debt presentation | Significant distress with atypical pattern | Biomarkers deviate but not in full PTSD Phenotype |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The Right to recognition of atypical circuit dysregulation patterns not captured by standard thresholds
2. The Right to culturally informed interpretation of autonomic and neuroendocrine stress responses
3. The Right to provisional classification when the temporal profile of circuit activation does not conform to standard windows.

### Unspecified Trauma/Stressor Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorder 309.9 [F43.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Stress-response circuit dysregulation present but insufficient information exists to map the specific pattern. Circuit analysis deferred.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The Right to provisional classification when forensic somatic data are insufficient for precise circuit mapping
2. The Right to re-evaluation when additional autonomic and neuroendocrine data become available.
