# Module 16: Substance-Related & Addictive Phenotypes

This module covers all substance-related and addictive phenotypes, reclassified from DSM-5 Substance-Related and Addictive Disorders. Each phenotype is re-interpreted through the lens of allostatic load, reward-circuit dysregulation, and neuro-somatic adaptation rather than moral or behavioral failure.

## 16.1 ALCOHOL

### Alcohol Use Phenotype (formerly Alcohol Use Disorder 303.90 [F10.20])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Chronic alcohol-induced dysregulation of GABA-glutamate homeostasis, mesolimbic dopamine pathway sensitization, and HPA axis disruption. Repeated intoxication-withdrawal cycles produce a progressive shift in reward set-point (opponent-process theory) such that alcohol consumption shifts from positive reinforcement (euphoria) to negative reinforcement (withdrawal relief). Hepatic enzyme induction, neurotoxicity (frontal lobe, cerebellum, Wernicke-Korsakoff vulnerability), and cardiovascular-pancreatic-gastrointestinal pathology index cumulative allostatic debt.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to toxicological verification: no substance use phenotype shall be assigned without biological confirmation of exposure.
2. The right to withdrawal safety: any substance with known withdrawal seizure or delirium risk requires medical detoxification supervision.
3. The right to polysubstance mapping: multiple substance involvement must be assessed; singlesubstance focus may misrepresent allostatic load.
4. The right to context-independent diagnosis: substance use during medical treatment (prescribed opioids, stimulants, benzodiazepines) producing tolerance/withdrawal alone does not constitute a use phenotype without other behavioral criteria.
