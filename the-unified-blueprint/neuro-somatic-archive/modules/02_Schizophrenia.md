# Module 02 — Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Phenotypes

This module reclassifies schizophrenia-spectrum disorders as disorders of salience-processing circuitry, wherein dopaminergic, glutamatergic, and GABAergic dysregulation produces fixed or transient impairments in reality testing. Each phenotype is mapped to its underlying allostatic architecture.

### Schizotypal Phenotype (formerly Schizotypal Personality Disorder

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Temporal-limbic dysgenesis with reduced gray matter volume in the left superior temporal gyrus and parahippocampal regions produces aberrant salience attribution to neutral stimuli. Mesocortical dopamine hypofunction coupled with subcortical hyperdopaminergia establishes a fixed pattern of referential ideation and perceptual distortions. Hippocampal volume reduction and altered P300 event-related potential latencies index a heritable neurodevelopmental trajectory that shares genetic liability with the schizophrenia spectrum. The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis demonstrates elevated basal cortisol with blunted dexamethasone suppression, reflecting chronic allostatic overload that consolidates eccentric behavioral ontogeny as a systemic adaptation rather than a discrete pathology.

### Delusional Phenotype (formerly Delusional Disorder 297.1)

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Striatal hyperdopaminergia confined predominantly to associative striatum produces fixed false beliefs without the global cognitive disorganization characteristic of broader-spectrum psychotic phenotypes. Prefrontal cortical dopamine D1 receptor-mediated working memory remains relatively intact, preserving the ability to maintain organized behavior outside the delusional system. The pathophysiology involves aberrant prediction-error signaling: mesolimbic dopamine neurons fire to expected-but-absent rewards, and the system assigns pathological salience to neutral events, locking the individual into a self-validating interpretive loop. Limbic kindling through chronic hypercortisolism progressively deepens delusional conviction by impairing hippocampal-dependent reality testing. The somatic architecture reveals no gross structural pathology; the phenotype represents a focal computational fault in belief-updating mechanisms rather than diffuse neural degeneration.

### Brief Psychotic Phenotype (formerly Brief Psychotic Disorder 298.8

**Allostatic Load Profile**

An acute allostatic surge precipitated by a discrete stressor triggers a transient hyperdopaminergic state in the mesolimbic pathway, producing rapid-onset hallucinations, delusions, or disorganization. The HPA axis releases corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH) that potentiates ventral tegmental area (VTA) dopamine neuron firing through CRH receptor type 1 activation. This stress-dopamine coupling creates a temporary salience dysregulation syndrome. The phenotype self-terminates within 1 month as cortisol negative feedback restores dopaminergic homeostasis and prefrontal inhibitory control re-engages. The capacity for full remission distinguishes this from phenotypes with fixed structural vulnerability. Somatic examination reveals no permanent neural architecture alteration; the episode represents a reversible computational state triggered by allostatic overload exceeding the individual’s stress-threshold ceiling.

### Schizophreniform Phenotype (formerly Schizophreniform Disorder

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Intermediate between the transient stress-dopamine surge of brief psychotic phenotypes and the fixed structural deterioration of schizophrenia. Striatal dopamine synthesis capacity is elevated but gray matter volume loss is absent or minimal, indicating a functional rather than degenerative pathophysiology. The 6-month time window captures individuals whose dopaminergic dysregulation is sustained enough to produce persistent psychotic symptoms but whose compensatory neural mechanisms remain partially operative. Approximately one-third of cases selfcorrect within the window, suggesting intact homeostatic re-regulation; the remaining two-thirds progress to the schizophrenia phenotype as dopamine-mediated excitotoxicity and inflammatory microglial activation begin to produce measurable cortical atrophy. The phenotype occupies a critical forensic window where intervention may determine whether the neurobiological trajectory resolves or hardens.

### Schizophrenia Phenotype (formerly Schizophrenia 295.90)

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A progressive neurodevelopmental encephalopathy characterized by irreversible alterations in cortical architecture: reduced gray matter volume in prefrontal and temporal cortices, ventricular enlargement, decreased dendritic arborization, and synaptic pruning beyond normative developmental limits. Striatal dopamine synthesis capacity is fixed at >1.3 times baseline, sustained by glutamatergic dysfunction at NMDA receptors on parvalbumin-positive GABA interneurons, which disinhibits cortical pyramidal neurons and drives excess subcortical dopamine release. Microglial activation and elevated inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-alpha) indicate ongoing neuroinflammatory contribution to white matter degradation. The 6-month continuous-signs criterion captures the transition from reversible functional dysregulation to structural systemic hardening. Anosognosia reflects right prefrontal-parietal network damage analogous to hemispatial neglect—a failure of the neural self-monitoring system rather than psychological denial.

### Schizoaffective Phenotype (formerly Schizoaffective Disorder

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A dual-pathway allostatic phenotype wherein both the dopaminergic (psychotic) and serotonergic/ noradrenergic (mood) regulatory systems operate outside homeostatic bounds. Striatal hyperdopaminergia produces the psychotic symptom cluster, while concurrent dysregulation of the HPA axis and serotonergic raphe nuclei drives the mood episode component. The requirement that mood episodes be present for the majority of total illness duration indicates that in this phenotype, mood circuit dysregulation is at least as biologically entrenched as the dopamine dysregulation. Central CRH hypersecretion drives both cortisol elevation and serotonergic dysfunction, creating a positive feedback loop: depression potentiates striatal dopamine sensitivity through glucocorticoid receptors on VTA neurons, while psychosis generates functional decline that deepens mood pathology. The phenotype occupies an intermediate position on the spectrum between schizophrenia and primary mood phenotypes, sharing genetic liability with both in polygenic risk score analyses.

### Substance/Medication-Induced Psychotic Phenotype (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Exogenous pharmacological agents directly hijack the brain’s dopamine, glutamate, and serotonin signaling systems, forcing neurotransmitter release, blocking reuptake, or sensitizing receptors beyond physiological limits. Each substance class has a characteristic neurobiological signature: stimulants (amphetamine, cocaine) provoke abrupt dopamine efflux from presynaptic terminals via reverse transport; cannabinoids agonize CB1 receptors on GABA interneurons, disinhibiting mesolimbic dopamine; NMDA antagonists (PCP, ketamine) block glutamate receptors on inhibitory interneurons, producing cortical disinhibition and subcortical dopamine surge; alcohol withdrawal kindles limbic circuits through GABA receptor downregulation. The distinction from primary psychotic phenotypes lies in the identifiable temporal relationship to substance exposure and the potential for full resolution upon elimination of the offending agent. Persistence beyond 1 month post-cessation indicates either irreversible neurotoxicity (e.g., methamphetamine-induced dopaminergic terminal damage) or unmasking of a latent primary psychotic phenotype.

### Psychotic Phenotype Due to Another Medical Condition (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A secondary psychotic syndrome arising from demonstrable structural, inflammatory, metabolic, or endocrine pathology that directly disrupts neural circuits mediating salience processing and perception. Unlike primary psychotic phenotypes where the pathophysiology originates within the dopaminergic system itself, here an external medical condition—temporal lobe epilepsy, autoimmune encephalitis (anti-NMDA receptor, anti-LGI1), CNS neoplasm, metabolic derangement (hypercortisolemia, hypoglycemia), endocrine disorder (Cushing’s, hyperthyroidism), or neurodegenerative disease—produces psychosis as a downstream effect. The olfactory hallucination is pathognomonic for temporal lobe seizure foci; visual hallucinations suggest occipital or brainstem pathology; complex multimodal hallucinations point to autoimmune limbic encephalitis. The forensic distinction hinges on identifying the medical condition through objective biomarkers: EEG, MRI lumbar puncture, endocrine panels. Treatment of the underlying condition is the primary intervention; psychotic symptoms may resolve or persist as a post-lesional scar.

### Catatonia Associated With Another Psychotic Phenotype (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Catatonia represents a state of extreme motor-intentional system failure wherein GABAergic inhibition within the basal ganglia-thalamo-cortical motor loop switches into either pathological hypoactivity (stupor, catalepsy) or pathological hyperactivity (agitation, stereotypy). The final common pathway involves GABA-A receptor hypofunction in the lateral orbitofrontal cortex and supplementary motor area, combined with relative dopamine D2 receptor blockade in the striatum. Glutamate hyperactivity via NMDA receptor disinhibition contributes to the motor agitation presentations. The 12 psychomotor signs represent differing expressions of this single circuit-level failure: motor inhibition (stupor, catalepsy, waxy flexibility) reflects excessive GABAergic output from the globus pallidus interna to the thalamus; motor disinhibition (agitation, stereotypy, mannerisms) reflects insufficient pallidal inhibition. The catatonia specifier is attached to the primary psychotic phenotype (schizophrenia, schizoaffective, bipolar) and signals a more severe allostatic decompensation requiring immediate medical intervention to prevent malignant progression (hyperthermia, autonomic instability, rhabdomyolysis).

### Catatonic Phenotype Due to Another Medical Condition (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Catatonia arising from a demonstrable medical etiology shares the same final common motorcircuit failure as catatonia associated with psychotic phenotypes—GABA-A hypofunction, dopamine D2 blockade, and NMDA receptor dysregulation in basal ganglia-thalamo-cortical loops—but here the root cause is an identifiable organic condition: autoimmune encephalitis (anti-NMDA receptor, anti-VGKC), metabolic derangement (hepatic encephalopathy at elevated ammonia levels, diabetic ketoacidosis, hypercalcemia), structural brain lesion (stroke, neoplasm, trauma), or toxic exposure (carbon monoxide, neuroleptic malignant syndrome). The critical forensic task is identifying the underlying medical condition through serum ammonia, calcium, glucose, autoimmune antibodies, CSF analysis, EEG, and neuroimaging. Resolution typically depends on treating the primary medical condition, though post-lesional catatonia may persist as a fixed deficit.
