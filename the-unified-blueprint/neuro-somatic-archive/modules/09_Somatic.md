# Module 09 — Somatic Symptom and Related Adaptations

Phenotypes from the Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6), reclassifying DSM-5 Somatic Symptom and Related Adaptations disorders through a neuro-somatic lens.

### Somatic Symptom Phenotype (formerly Somatic Symptom Disorder 300.82 [F45.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Chronic hyper-vigilant interoceptive state with sustained sympathetic activation, elevated baseline cortisol, reduced vagal tone, and amplified insular-anterior cingulate gain on visceral afferent signals. The organism maintains a persistent threat-detection posture toward internal bodily sensations despite absent or minimal structural pathology.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A. Distressing somatic symptoms disrupting daily life | Sustained interoceptive hypervigilance with visceral hypersensitivity | Insular and ACC gain amplification on visceral afferents |
| B1. Disproportionate thoughts about symptom seriousness | Prefrontal-limbic disinhibition; catastrophizing appraisal circuit | Amygdalar hyperreactivity with reduced vmPFC regulatory control |
| B2. Persistently high health- related anxiety | Chronic sympathetic arousal without parasympathetic counterregulation | Elevated norepinephrine; HPA axis hyperactivity |
| B3. Excessive time/energy on symptoms | Attentional fixation with narrowed behavioral repertoire | Insula-ACC salience network hyperconnectivity |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to interoceptive calibration free from iatrogenic amplification of visceral signals.
2. The right to therapeutic interventions that do not induce paradoxical nociceptive sensitization.
3. The right to clinical distinction between allostatic overload and structural pathology.
4. The right to somatic therapies that restore vagal tone rather than entrench hypervigilance.

### Somatic Symptom Phenotype with Predominant Pain (formerly Somatic Symptom Disorder with Predominant Pain 300.82 [F45.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

4. The right to assessment that identifies spinothalamic gain as a modifiable biological parameter.

### Functional Neurological Symptom Phenotype (formerly Conversion Disorder / Functional Neurological Symptom Disorder 300.11 [F44.x])

Motor-sensory circuit dissociation with impaired volitional pathway recruitment, preserved automatic/intact function, and stress-induced functional network decoupling. ### Factitious Phenotype Imposed on Another (formerly Factitious Disorder Imposed on Another 300.19 [F68.10])

Caregiver sick-role-by-proxy reward circuitry with vicarious ventral striatal activation through medical attention directed at a dependent. The perpetrator’s allostatic state is regulated by the intensity of medical intervention on the victim, with escalation driven by habituation. The victim’s physiology is directly compromised.

### Unspecified Somatic Symptom Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Somatic Symptom and Related Disorder 300.82 [F45.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Uncharacterized somatic allostatic state with insufficient information for categorical classification. The organism presents with somatic distress not yet mapped to a specific neuro-somatic circuit.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to a provisional classification without premature commitment to a specific neurosomatic circuit.
2. The right to ongoing assessment as biological markers clarify.
3. The right to symptom management regardless of classification certainty.
4. The right to reclassification when sufficient biological data are obtained.
