# Module 13 — Sexual Phenotypes

This module reclassifies sexual dysfunctions as disruptions of specific neuro-somatic circuits governing desire, arousal, orgasm, ejaculation, and pain perception. Each phenotype maps to a distinct circuit node failure — vascular, neurological, endocrine, or muscular — with objective biomarkers replacing subjective categorical thresholds.

### 13.1 Delayed Ejaculation Phenotype (formerly Delayed Ejaculation 302.74 [F52.32])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Decreased fast-conducting peripheral sensory nerve density in the pudendal and hypogastric pathways coupled with age-related sex steroid decline (testosterone, DHT). Autonomic dysregulation at the lumbar sympathetic ganglia reduces bulbospongiosus reflex arc gain. Serotonergic hypertonicity (5-HT2C receptor overactivation in the spinal ejaculation generator) raises the ejaculatory threshold.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Marked delay in ejaculation | Increased latency of bulbospongiosus reflex arc | Pudendal nerve conduction velocity reduction; spinal ejaculation generator inhibition |
| A2: Marked infrequency/absence of ejaculation | Failure of sympathetic-propelled seminal emission | Hypogastric nerve (sympathetic chain T10–L2) insufficient output |
| B: 6-month persistence | Chronification of neural gating | Peripheral sensory nerve demyelination or age-related attrition |
| C: Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive alarm signaling | Anterior cingulate cortex hyperactivation from failed reproductive reward prediction |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Bulbocavernosus reflex latency | Perineal electrode stimulation with EMG recording at bulbospongiosus | Latency >40 ms |
| Nocturnal ejaculation frequency | Self-report diary over 4 weeks | 0 events over 28 days |
| Serum prolactin (fasting AM) | Venipuncture, chemiluminescence | >20 ng/mL |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Ejaculation within 5–10 min of partnered activity; no distress | IELT >3 min; normal bulbocavernosus reflex; prolactin <15 ng/mL |
| Transition Zone | Occasional delay (25–50% of encounters); mild distress | IELT 1–3 min; prolonged but present nocturnal emissions; prolactin 15–20 ng/mL |
| Somatic Debt | Delay on >75% of encounters; significant distress | IELT consistently absent or >20 min; absent nocturnal emissions; prolactin >20 ng/mL |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The bulbocavernosus reflex arc has the right to fire within its species-typical latency window without supraspinal serotonergic override.
2. The hypogastric sympathetic chain has the right to unimpeded signal conduction through the lumbar ganglia without pharmacological or surgical interruption.
3. The spinal ejaculation generator has the right to a dopamine:serotonin ratio that does not pathologically elevate its firing threshold.
4. The peripheral sensory afferents of the glans penis have the right to age-appropriate nerve conduction velocity free of compression, metabolic, or toxic insult.

### 13.2 Erectile Phenotype (formerly Erectile Disorder 302.72 [F52.21])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS) downregulation in the corpus cavernosum sinusoidal endothelium reduces cyclic GMP-mediated smooth muscle relaxation. Autonomic imbalance — sympathetic overdrive and parasympathetic (pelvic splanchnic, S2–S4) underdrive — restricts helicine artery dilation. Cavernosal fibrosis from chronic ischemia or TGF-β1 upregulation limits lacunar compliance.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Difficulty obtaining erection | Failure of parasympathetic-driven helicine artery vasodilation | Pelvic splanchnic nerve (S2–S4) low acetylcholine output; eNOS uncoupling |
| A2: Difficulty maintaining erection | Veno-occlusive mechanism failure from inadequate trabecular smooth muscle relaxation | Corpus cavernosum fibroelastic distension deficit; tunica albuginea compliance loss |
| A3: Decrease in erectile rigidity | Insufficient intracavernosal pressure relative to systolic BP | Cavernosal artery stenosis or TGF-β1-mediated collagen deposition |
| B: 6-month persistence | Structural remodeling of erectile tissue compliance cycle | Chronic hypoxia → fibrosis → reduced compliance |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Nocturnal penile tumescence (NPT) | RigiScan monitoring over 3 nights | <60% base rigidity for <10 min per event |
| Cavernosal artery PSV | Doppler ultrasound after intracavernosal PGE1 injection | PSV <30 cm/s |
| Fasting serum glucose + lipid panel | Venipuncture, enzymatic assay | Glucose >100 mg/dL; LDL >130 mg/dL |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Erection sufficient for penetration and completion on >75% of attempts | NPT rigidity >70%; PSV >35 cm/s; eNOS function intact |
| Transition Zone | Occasional failure (25–50%); situational | NPT rigidity 50–70%; PSV 30–35 cm/s; intermittent nocturnal erections |
| Somatic Debt | Failure on >75% of occasions; 6+ months | NPT rigidity <50%; PSV <30 cm/s; absent nocturnal erections; arterial calcification present |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The corpus cavernosum sinusoidal endothelium has the right to sufficient eNOS activity to generate adequate cGMP for smooth muscle relaxation.
2. The pelvic splanchnic parasympathetic outflow (S2–S4) has the right to signal without sympathetic override from the hypogastric nerve.
3. The tunica albuginea has the right to age-appropriate compliance free of TGF-β1-driven fibrotic remodeling.
4. The helicine arteries have the right to unimpeded luminal flow without atherosclerotic plaque or endothelial dysfunction.

### 13.3 Female Orgasmic Phenotype (formerly Female Orgasmic Disorder 302.73 [F52.31])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Clitoral dorsal nerve afferent conduction insufficiency combined with pelvic floor neuromuscular discoordination. Reduced activation of the mesolimbic dopamine reward circuit (nucleus accumbens — ventral tegmental area) during sexual stimulation. SSRI-mediated 5-HT2C receptor hyperactivation in the periaqueductal gray raises orgasmic threshold. Pelvic nerve (parasympathetic) and hypogastric nerve (sympathetic) discoordination prevents the synchronized genital-uterine reflex arc required for orgasmic release.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Marked delay/infrequency/absence of orgasm | Elevated genito-spinal reflex threshold | Dorsal clitoral nerve reduced conduction; spinal orgasmic generator hypofunction |
| A2: Reduced intensity of orgasmic sensations | Attenuated pelvic floor neuromuscular recruitment | Pubococcygeus muscle weak contractile duration; pudendal nerve motor branch attenuation |
| B: 6-month persistence | Long-loop afferent habituation | Mesolimbic dopamine pathway desensitization |
| C: Clinically significant distress | Reward prediction error signaling | Anterior insula and ACC hyperactivation from non-reward |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Clitoral vibratory threshold | Biothesiometer applied to dorsal clitoral surface | Threshold >3.5 μm amplitude |
| Pelvic floor muscle endurance | Perineometer; sustained contraction duration | Sustained contraction <5 seconds |
| Serum free testosterone | Venipuncture, equilibrium dialysis | <0.5 pg/mL (premenopausal); <0.2 pg/mL (postmenopausal) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Orgasmic on >75% of partnered encounters | Clitoral threshold <2.5 μm; pelvic floor endurance >10 s |
| Transition Zone | Orgasmic 25–75% of encounters; situational | Threshold 2.5–3.5 μm; endurance 5–10 s |
| Somatic Debt | Orgasmic on <25% or never; 6+ months | Threshold >3.5 μm; endurance <5 s; absent nocturnal orgasmic sensations |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The dorsal clitoral nerve has the right to transmit mechanosensory afference to the spinal orgasmic generator without conduction block.
2. The pelvic floor musculature has the right to execute sustained rhythmic contraction sequences without neuromuscular fatigue.
3. The mesolimbic dopamine reward system has the right to receive orgasmic reward prediction signals without serotonergic suppression.
4. The pudendal nerve motor branches have the right to full recruitment of the pubococcygeus muscle without iatrogenic pharmacological attenuation.

### 13.4 Female Sexual Interest/Arousal Phenotype (formerly Female Sexual Interest/Arousal Disorder 302.72 [F52.22])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian (HPO) axis underactivation reduces estradiol and free testosterone, lowering central dopaminergic tone in the medial preoptic area (MPOA). Vaginal vasocongestion deficit from reduced pelvic parasympathetic (pelvic nerve) signaling. Reduced nitric oxide synthase activity in vaginal and clitoral erectile tissue.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Reduced interest in sexual activity | Low MPOA dopamine drive to appetitive behavior circuits | Hypoestrogenism; low free testosterone; HPO axis suppression |
| A2: Reduced sexual/erotic thoughts | Prefrontal-limbic disconnection for sexual cue salience | Medial prefrontal cortex hypoactivation to erotic cues |
| A3: No initiation/unreceptive to partner | Reduced HPA synergy for sexual approach behavior | Corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH) dysregulation |
| A4: Reduced excitement/pleasure | Deficient genital vasocongestion | Vaginal epithelial NO synthase low output |
| A5: Reduced response to erotic cues | Sensory gating failure in the amygdala-nucleus accumbens pathway | Low dopaminergic salience tagging of erotic stimuli |
| A6: Reduced genital/nongenital sensations | Vaginal mechanoreceptor desensitization | Reduced pelvic blood flow; vaginal atrophy |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Serum estradiol (early follicular) | Venipuncture, LC-MS/MS | <30 pg/mL |
| Vaginal pH | pH test strip on lateral vaginal wall | pH >5.0 (indicating atrophy) |
| Free testosterone | Venipuncture, equilibrium dialysis | <0.3 pg/mL (premenopausal) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Interest in >75% of sexual opportunities | Estradiol >50 pg/mL; free T >0.5 pg/mL; vaginal pH <4.5 |
| Transition Zone | Interest 25–75% of opportunities | Estradiol 30–50 pg/mL; free T 0.3–0.5 pg/mL; pH 4.5–5.0 |
| Somatic Debt | Interest <25% of opportunities; 6+ months | Estradiol <30 pg/mL; free T <0.3 pg/mL; pH >5.0 |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The HPO axis has the right to generate estradiol levels sufficient to maintain vaginal epithelial integrity and central dopaminergic tone.
2. The pelvic nerve has the right to transmit parasympathetic vasodilatory signals to the vaginal and clitoral erectile tissue without pharmacological blockade.
3. The medial preoptic area has the right to receive dopaminergic appetitive drive signals without HPA axis interference.
4. The vaginal mechanoreceptors have the right to estrogen-mediated sensitivity within the species-typical range for reproductive-age tissue.

### 13.5 Genito-Pelvic Pain/Penetration Phenotype (formerly Genito-Pelvic Pain/Penetration Disorder 302.76 [F52.6])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Pelvic floor hypertonicity with elevated EMG activity in the pubococcygeus and levator ani muscles. Vulvovaginal atrophy from hypoestrogenism reduces mucosal barrier integrity. Peripheral nociceptor sensitization (C-fiber and Aδ-fiber) in the pudendal nerve distribution. Central sensitization via spinal dorsal horn wind-up and thalamocortical amplification.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Difficulty with vaginal penetration | Mechanical obstruction from pelvic floor hypertonus | Levator ani and pubococcygeus sustained contraction >60% of max voluntary |
| A2: Vulvovaginal/pelvic pain | Nociceptor activation in the vestibule and pelvic viscera | Pudendal nerve C-fiber sensitization; vulvar vestibular mechanoreceptor allodynia |
| A3: Fear/anxiety about pain | Conditioned anticipatory nociception | Amygdala-hippocampal threat memory consolidation; insular hyperactivation |
| A4: Pelvic floor muscle tensing | Inappropriate guarding reflex of the pelvic floor | Sacral reflex arc (S2–S4) disinhibition of the levator ani motor neurons |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Pelvic floor EMG resting baseline | Intravaginal surface EMG probe | >5 μV at rest |
| Vulvar vestibular cotton-swab test | Palpation of vestibular gland openings with Q-tip | Pain score >3/10 at 3+ sites |
| Vaginal maturation index (parabasal:intermediate:superficial ratio) | Vaginal smear cytology | Parabasal cells >20% |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Pain-free penetration >75% of attempts | Resting EMG <2 μV; VMI shows >50% superficial cells; swab test negative |
| Transition Zone | Pain on 25–75% of attempts; mild avoidance | Resting EMG 2–5 μV; VMI 20–50% superficial; 1–2 positive swab sites |
| Somatic Debt | Pain >75% of attempts; complete avoidance; 6+ months | Resting EMG >5 μV; VMI <20% superficial; 3+ positive swab sites |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The levator ani and pubococcygeus have the right to resting tone within the species-typical relaxation range without sustained guarding.
2. The vulvar vestibular mucosa has the right to estrogen-mediated barrier integrity free of nociceptor sensitization.
3. The pudendal nerve C-fiber population has the right to a resting activation threshold that does not produce allodynia at light touch.
4. The sacral spinal reflex arc (S2–S4) has the right to discriminate penetrative intent from threat without conditioned guarding.

### 13.6 Male Hypoactive Sexual Desire Phenotype (formerly Male Hypoactive Sexual Desire Disorder 302.71 [F52.0])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis underactivation reduces bioavailable testosterone below the individual's critical threshold for libido. Hyperprolactinemia suppresses GnRH pulsatility and central dopaminergic tone at the medial preoptic area (MPOA). Reduced melanocortin (α-MSH) signaling at the MC4 receptor in the paraventricular nucleus.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A: Deficient sexual/erotic thoughts and desire | Insufficient MPOA dopaminergic appetitive drive | Low free testosterone; high prolactin; reduced α-MSH/MC4 signaling |
| B: 6-month persistence | HPG axis chronic downregulation | Impaired LH pulsatility from pituitary or hypothalamic suppression |
| C: Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive mismatch between expected and actual drive | Anterior cingulate cortex — insula circuitry detection of drive deficit |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Total testosterone (AM, fasted) | Venipuncture, LC-MS/MS | <300 ng/dL |
| Prolactin (fasting AM) | Venipuncture, chemiluminescence | >20 ng/mL |
| LH + FSH | Venipuncture, immunoassay | LH <1.5 IU/L (hypogonadotropic pattern) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Spontaneous sexual thoughts ≥ weekly; responsive to partner initiation | Total T >400 ng/dL; prolactin <15 ng/mL; LH 1.5–8 IU/L |
| Transition Zone | Thoughts < monthly; minimal initiation | T 300–400 ng/dL; prolactin 15–20 ng/mL |
| Somatic Debt | Absent spontaneous thoughts; no initiation for 6+ months | T <300 ng/dL; prolactin >20 ng/mL; LH suppressed |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The HPG axis has the right to generate testosterone levels sufficient for MPOA dopaminergic drive to appetitive sexual behavior.
2. The pituitary lactotrophs have the right to operate below the threshold of GnRH suppression (prolactin <20 ng/mL).
3. The MC4 receptor in the paraventricular nucleus has the right to receive α-MSH signaling for sexual motivation without antagonism.
4. The medial preoptic area has the right to unimpaired dopaminergic transmission for cue salience tagging of sexual stimuli.

### 13.7 Premature Ejaculation Phenotype (formerly Premature [Early] Ejaculation 302.75 [F52.4])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Hypersensitivity of the ejaculatory reflex arc: reduced 5-HT2C/5-HT1B serotonin receptor density in the spinal ejaculation generator leads to lower inhibitory tone. Hyposensitivity of central 5-HT1A autoreceptors reduces serotonergic negative feedback. Hyperdopaminergic tone in the ventral tegmental area — nucleus accumbens pathway lowers the ejaculatory threshold. Increased nerve conduction velocity in the dorsal penile nerve afferents.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A: Ejaculation within ~1 min of vaginal penetration | Low spinal ejaculation generator threshold | Reduced 5-HT2C/5-HT1B receptor binding in the spinal cord; high dorsal penile nerve conduction velocity |
| B: 75–100% of occasions over 6 months | Fixed low-threshold reflex arc | Serotonin transporter (SLC6A4) polymorphism; low central 5-HT tone |
| C: Clinically significant distress | Reward truncation signaling | Mesolimbic dopamine reward circuit incomplete satiation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Intravaginal ejaculatory latency time (IELT) | Self-report or partner stopwatch over 4 weeks | Mean IELT <60 seconds |
| Dorsal penile nerve conduction velocity | Surface electrode stimulation at penile base; recording at glans | Velocity >35 m/s |
| Premature Ejaculation Diagnostic Tool (PEDT) | 5-item standardized questionnaire | Score >11 |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | IELT >3 min; subjective control | Dorsal nerve velocity <30 m/s; PEDT <8 |
| Transition Zone | IELT 1–3 min; occasional control loss | Velocity 30–35 m/s; PEDT 8–11 |
| Somatic Debt | IELT <60 s on >75% of encounters; 6+ months | Velocity >35 m/s; PEDT >11; absent control |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The spinal ejaculation generator has the right to a 5-HT2C/5-HT1B receptor density that provides adequate inhibitory tone to maintain ejaculatory latency within species-typical range.
2. The dorsal penile nerve afferents have the right to conduction velocity within the normal range (<35 m/s) without supranormal amplification.
3. The serotonergic raphe-spinal pathway has the right to sufficient 5-HT availability for tonic inhibition of the ejaculatory reflex.
4. The mesolimbic dopamine reward system has the right to a complete satiation arc before reflex termination.

### 13.8 Substance/Medication-Induced Sexual Dysfunction Phenotype (formerly Substance/Medication-Induced Sexual Dysfunction 292.89 [various])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Pharmacological disruption of one or more nodes in the sexual response circuit: dopaminergic (antipsychotics, stimulant withdrawal), serotonergic (SSRIs, MAOIs), endocrine (prolactin elevation from antipsychotics; testosterone suppression from opioids), or autonomic (anticholinergic, antihypertensive agents). The specific mechanism depends on the agent class but converges on reduced neurovascular or neuroendocrine signaling.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A: Clinically significant sexual function disturbance | Target receptor/system blockade in sexual circuit | Specific to agent (e.g., SSRI → 5-HT2C spinal inhibition; antipsychotic → prolactin → GnRH suppression) |
| B: Temporal relationship to substance/medication | Xenobiotic perturbation of homeostatic signaling | Receptor occupancy; neurotransmitter depletion; endocrine feedback loop disruption |
| E: Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive detection of circuit failure | Anterior cingulate — insula network alarm signaling |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Temporal drug-event correlation | Naranjo Adverse Drug Reaction Probability Scale | Score >4 (probable) |
| Serum prolactin (if on antipsychotic) | Venipuncture, chemiluminescence | >25 ng/mL |
| Discontinuation challenge | Supervised dose reduction or switch trial | Symptom improvement within 2 weeks |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Normal sexual function on current regimen | No temporal drug-event correlation |
| Transition Zone | Mild dysfunction on 25–50% of occasions | Naranjo score 1–4 (possible); prolactin 20–25 ng/mL |
| Somatic Debt | Moderate-severe dysfunction on >50% of occasions; Naranjo score >4 | Prolactin >25 ng/mL; positive discontinuation challenge |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The neuroendocrine sexual circuit has the right to operate free of iatrogenic receptor blockade sufficient to suppress function below species-typical thresholds.
2. The pituitary-gonadal axis has the right to prolactin levels below the threshold of GnRH suppression regardless of antipsychotic exposure.
3. The autonomic pelvic outflow has the right to intact cholinergic and nitrergic signaling without pharmacological antagonism.
4. The spinal ejaculatory/orgasmic generator has the right to a serotonin:dopamine ratio that does not pathologically elevate or lower its firing threshold.

### 13.9 Other Specified Sexual Dysfunction Phenotype (formerly 302.79 [F52.8])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Subthreshold disruption of one or more sexual circuit nodes (desire, arousal, orgasm, pain) that does not meet the full threshold for a specific phenotype but produces sufficient allostatic load to generate distress. Presentation-specific neurobiological signature must be identified on a case-by-case basis (e.g., sexual aversion: amygdala hyperreactivity to sexual cues with elevated sympathetic tone; anhedonic ejaculation: intact ejaculation with deficient mesolimbic dopamine reward response).

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of sexual dysfunction not meeting full criteria | Partial circuit perturbation insufficient to cross categorical threshold | Variable — requires individual circuit mapping |
| Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive detection of subthreshold circuit failure | Anterior insula — ACC network activation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Individual symptom mapping | Structured clinical interview + targeted biomarker | Depends on symptom domain |
| Rule-out of known phenotypes | Diagnostic algorithm per above sections | Negative on all specific phenotype criteria |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No distressing sexual symptoms | Normal on all domain-specific proxies |
| Transition Zone | Mild symptoms failing to meet full criteria | Partial elevation on one domain biomarker |
| Somatic Debt | Distressing symptoms present but below full threshold | Domain-specific biomarker above normal but below phenotype cut-off |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The sexual circuit has the right to function without subthreshold signal disruption that nonetheless produces distress.
2. The individual has the right to clinical attention for sexual circuit dysfunction regardless of whether a categorical threshold is crossed.
3. The interoceptive network has the right to accurate threat assessment without false-positive alarm signaling from partial circuit perturbation.
4. Each sexual circuit node has the right to a case-specific assessment rather than forced categorical assignment.

### 13.10 Unspecified Sexual Dysfunction Phenotype (formerly 302.70 [F52.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Sexual circuit disruption of unspecified neurobiological origin where insufficient data exist to map the specific circuit node. The absence of mechanistic specification does not indicate absence of somatic substrate — only that the substrate has not been localized.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of sexual dysfunction not meeting full criteria | Unmapped circuit perturbation | Unknown pending further data collection |
| Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive detection of unmapped circuit failure | Anterior insula — ACC activation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive circuit workup | Panel of all domain-specific biomarkers from above sections | Pending results |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Normal function | All biomarkers within normal range |
| Somatic Debt | Distressing symptoms without identified mechanism | Biomarker workup incomplete or inconclusive |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The individual has the right to clinical recognition of sexual circuit distress even when the precise somatic origin has not been localized.
2. The absence of a mapped mechanism shall not be grounds for dismissing the biological reality of the dysfunction.
3. The sexual circuit has the right to comprehensive investigation rather than arbitrary categorical assignment.
4. The individual has the right to somatic intervention based on circuit-level logic even before precise node identification.
