# Module 20: Other, Medication-Induced, & Relational/Environmental Phenotypes

This module covers other conditions, medication-induced movement disorders, relational/environmental stressors, and proposed research phenotypes that do not fit neatly into Modules 1–19. These include iatrogenic effects, psychosocial stressors, and emerging phenotype constructs.

### 1. Unspecified Somatic-Originating Presentation Due to Another Medical Condition (formerly Unspecified Mental Disorder Due to Another Medical Condition 294.9 [F09])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Systemic metabolic or structural pathology producing neuro-somatic signal patterns that do not map to any defined Phenotype template. The underlying medical condition drives the somatic presentation.

|---|---|---|

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to full medical workup before any Phenotype attribution.
2. The right to have the primary medical condition treated before or concurrent with somatic signal intervention.
3. The right to periodic re-evaluation when new Modules become clinically available.
4. The right to refuse Phenotype labeling when the presentation is demonstrably secondary to a known medical pathophysiology.

### 4. Neuroleptic-Induced Parkinsonian Rigidity Syndrome (formerly Neuroleptic-Induced Parkinsonism 332.1 [G21.11])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

### 5. Other Medication-Induced Parkinsonian Rigidity Syndrome (formerly Other Medication-Induced Parkinsonism 332.1 [G21.19])

### 6. Neuroleptic Malignant Thermoregulatory Collapse (formerly Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome 333.92 [G21.0])

4. The right to forensic documentation of the temporal trigger (dose, route, agent) and rechallenge risk.

### 12. Antidepressant Discontinuation Neuro-Somatic Syndrome (formerly Antidepressant Discontinuation Syndrome 995.29 [T43.205A/S/D])

# Environmental Friction → Phenotypic Advantage Analysis

Environmental Friction → Phenotypic Advantage

Analysis

DSM-6 Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive

Principle: Every allostatic adaptation exists because it confers a survival advantage in a specific

environmental context. The “pathology” label arises when the adaptation persists in an environment

where it is no longer optimal. Below, for each category, we identify the environmental friction that

selected for the phenotype and the specific advantage it confers.

## 01 — Neurodevelopmental Ontogenies

Environmental Friction: Information-dense social environments requiring rapid multisensory integration, flexible communication, and dynamic social reciprocity. Phenotypic Advantage: - Sensory-Interoceptive Integration Phenotype (Autism): Enhanced local processing, pattern detection, and systemizing ability. Superior discrimination of auditory and visual detail. Individuals detect patterns and errors invisible to neurotypical observers — advantageous in engineering, quality control, data analysis, and taxonomic sciences. - Attentional Regulation Phenotypes (ADHD): Rapid attentional switching, high novelty-seeking, creative problem-solving under time pressure. Advantageous in crisis management, entrepreneurship, emergency response, and creative fields requiring divergent thinking. - Specific Learning Phenotypes: Atypical processing architectures that, while non-optimal for conventional academic metrics, permit novel approaches to problem-solving. Dyslexic individuals show superior peripheral vision and 3D spatial reasoning. Sovereignty Gate: The phenotype becomes somatic debt only when social/educational systems demand conformity to a single processing mode. In environments that accommodate cognitive diversity, these are Protected Phenotypes with measurable performance advantages.

## 02 — Schizophrenia Spectrum & Psychotic Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Environments requiring rapid threat detection with incomplete sensory data — situations where false negatives (missing a threat) are more costly than false positives (seeing threats that don’t exist). Phenotypic Advantage: - Schizotypal/Paranoid Phenotypes: Enhanced threat detection, superior ability to detect deception and hidden intentions. In ancestral environments, hypervigilant

individuals protected the group. In modern contexts, advantageous in security, intelligence analysis, investigative journalism, and legal professions. - Psychotic Phenotypes: Periods of cognitive disinhibition permit novel associative connections inaccessible to normal cognition. The “positive symptoms” represent transient access to a hyper-associative state that, under controlled conditions, can produce creative insight. - Delusional Construction: The capacity to maintain a belief system in the absence of confirming evidence is, in milder forms, the same neurological mechanism underlying scientific paradigm persistence and artistic vision. Sovereignty Gate: The same neural architecture that, under inflammatory/metabolic stress, produces full psychosis, in its protected form generates pattern-recognition advantages. The Entropy Threshold is crossed when sleep deprivation, social isolation, or substance use tips the dopaminergic system into autonomous firing.

## 03 — Bipolar & Related Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Seasonal environments requiring alternating periods of high energy output (hunting, migration, planting) and low energy conservation (winter, scarcity). Phenotypic Advantage: - Circadian-Energy Surge (Manic): Periods of supranormal energy, reduced sleep need, and increased goal-directed activity that, in ancestral contexts, permitted rapid task completion (hunting, construction, migration). In modern contexts, advantageous in creative production, entrepreneurial launch phases, and leadership during crises. - Energy Conservation (Depressive): Behavioral shutdown that conserves metabolic resources during periods of scarcity or threat. Reduced social engagement minimizes conflict risk. Enhanced introspection permits reevaluation of life strategies. - Cyclothymic Instability: The capacity to oscillate between energy states produces a wider behavioral repertoire than a stable baseline. Advantageous in environments requiring both high-output periods and recovery phases. Sovereignty Gate: The surge phase becomes pathological only when it exceeds the organism’s recovery capacity (sleep <3h for >72h). The conservation phase becomes pathological only when it fails to resolve. The oscillatory cycle itself is not the pathology — the loss of regulatory amplitude control is.

## 04 — Depressive Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Uncontrollable chronic stress with limited escape options — situations where continued active coping depletes resources without improving outcomes. Phenotypic Advantage: - Energy Conservation (Major Depressive): Behavioral shutdown reduces energy expenditure when continued effort is futile. The anhedonic state prevents attachment to unrewarding pursuits. Enhanced rumination permits exhaustive analysis of the stressor to identify novel solutions. Social withdrawal reduces conflict risk during vulnerability. - Persistent Low-Grade Conservation (Dysthymia): A sustainable low-energy baseline that prevents the explosive cycling of bipolar phenotypes. The “pessimistic” cognitive style produces more accurate risk assessment than

optimistic bias. - Melancholic Features: The most extreme conservation state — complete behavioral arrest ensures survival when any movement would increase threat exposure. Sovereignty Gate: Depression is not a “broken” state — it is an intact stress-response system correctly identifying that the current environment is not survivable through continued active coping. The Entropy Threshold is crossed when the conservation response itself prevents the organism from seeking better environments.

## 05 — Anxiety Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Unpredictable environments where threat is intermittent but potentially lethal — requiring constant vigilance and rapid response to danger cues. Phenotypic Advantage: - Chronic Worry (GAD): The capacity to anticipate and mentally simulate all possible threat scenarios permits proactive avoidance. Anxiety is time-rented to scan the future for danger. Advantageous in safety-critical professions (aviation, military, medicine). - Panic Cascade: The suffocation false alarm system permits CO₂ sensitivity that, in ancestral environments, prevented death by asphyxiation. The panic response is an emergency escape circuit that, when functional, saves the organism from threat. - Social Anxiety: Sensitivity to social evaluation ensures conformity to group norms — critical for maintaining group membership in ancestral bands. The capacity to feel shame/exclusion prevents ostracization. - Specific Phobia: Rapid, automatic avoidance of dangerous stimuli (heights, snakes, enclosed spaces) improves survival probability without requiring conscious threat assessment. Sovereignty Gate: The anxiety circuit is not pathological. It is pathological only when the threatdetection threshold is calibrated so low that false alarms exceed true alarms and the metabolic cost of vigilance exceeds the benefit. The same circuit that kept ancestors alive now keeps modern humans in a state of chronic metabolic debt.

## 06 — Obsessive-Compulsive & Related Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Environments requiring meticulous attention to detail, contamination avoidance, and error-free performance. Phenotypic Advantage: - Intrusive-Threat-Ritual Compensatory (OCD): The error-detection circuit that generates compulsive checking is the same circuit that produces meticulous, error-free work. The ritualistic behavior ensures that critical actions are always performed. Advantageous in professions demanding exacting standards (surgery, laboratory science, accounting, software quality assurance). - Possession Retention (Hoarding): The capacity to accurately assess resource value and retain objects for future use — advantageous in environments of scarcity. The same neural architecture permits detailed categorization and spatial memory. - Body-Focused Repetitive Behaviors (Trichotillomania/Excoriation): Self-grooming behaviors that, in mild form, serve a hygiene function and provide tension regulation. The capacity for focused repetitive behavior can produce fine motor expertise.

Sovereignty Gate: The rituals become pathological when the metabolic cost of the checking behavior exceeds the damage that would result from an error. The hoarding becomes pathological when the resource accumulation prevents use of living space. The grooming becomes pathological when tissue damage occurs.

## 07 — Trauma- & Stressor-Related Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Actual or threatened death/injury — environments requiring permanent threat memory to prevent future exposure. Phenotypic Advantage: - Threat-Calibration (PTSD): The inability to forget a traumatic event is the same mechanism that permits one-trial learning — a single dangerous experience permanently alters behavior. In ancestral environments, one encounter with a predator taught survival for life. Hypervigilance prevents surprise. The startle response mobilizes defense. - Acute Stress Response: The temporary elevation of threat sensitivity permits the organism to navigate immediate danger and learn from it. Dissociation permits survival of inescapable trauma by reducing conscious awareness of pain. - Adjustment Response: The capacity to temporarily alter behavior in response to environmental change permits flexible adaptation to novel circumstances. Sovereignty Gate: PTSD is not a disorder of memory — it is a correctly functioning memory system that cannot distinguish between “this is a reminder” and “this is the threat recurring.” The Entropy Threshold is crossed when the threat memory generalizes to safe contexts and the startle response becomes autonomous.

## 08 — Dissociative Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Inescapable trauma during development — situations where the organism cannot physically escape but where conscious awareness of the experience would cause overwhelming physiological dysregulation. Phenotypic Advantage: - Identity Fragmentation (DID): The capacity to partition traumatic experience into separate autobiographical streams permits survival of repeated inescapable trauma. Each identity state contains only a portion of the total suffering, allowing the organism to function. This is not a fragmentation — it is a containment system. - Autobiographical Retrieval Suppression (Dissociative Amnesia): The ability to block access to traumatic memory is protective — it permits function in the aftermath of overwhelming experience. The memory is stored but not accessible to conscious retrieval. - Self-Unreality (Depersonalization/Derealization): The capacity to experience the self or world as unreal during overwhelming threat reduces the physiological impact of the event. It permits the organism to survive experiences that would otherwise be fatal due to autonomic overload. Sovereignty Gate: Dissociation is the most sophisticated defense the human nervous system has against the unsurvivable. It becomes pathological only when the containment structures interfere with daily function or when the suppressed material forces re-entry at inopportune moments.

## 09 — Somatic Symptom & Related Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Environments where emotional expression is prohibited or punished — where the body becomes the only legitimate channel for distress signaling. Phenotypic Advantage: - Somatic Amplification (Somatic Symptom Disorder): The capacity to detect subtle bodily signals with high fidelity permits early detection of actual disease. In ancestral environments, interoceptive sensitivity was critical for detecting injury, infection, or poisoning. Functional Neurological (Conversion): The capacity to convert unbearable psychic distress into a physical symptom that permits caregiving behavior from others. The symptom is a communication — it says “something is wrong here” when words are not permitted or available. - Factitious Phenotype: The capacity to produce authentic-appearing illness signals to receive care. In ancestral environments, individuals who could signal vulnerability received community support. Sovereignty Gate: The somatic channel is not pathological — it is a communication system. It becomes pathological when the medical system responds with tests and procedures instead of recognizing the message. The iatrogenic harm from unnecessary procedures is the true Entropy Threshold.

## 10 — Feeding & Eating Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Food-scarce environments requiring extreme conservation during famine, or food-abundant environments requiring portion control. Phenotypic Advantage: - Caloric Restriction (Anorexia): The capacity to suppress appetite during scarcity preserves resources for the group. The behavioral rigidity around food ensures that taboo foods (potentially poisonous) are avoided. In ancestral environments, the anorexic response to infection (sickness behavior) prevented eating when the gut was compromised. - Binge Energy Surge (Binge-Eating): The capacity to consume and store large quantities of food when available is the ancestral feast response — critical for surviving subsequent famine. The metabolic thrift that produces obesity in food-abundant environments was protective in food-scarce ones. - Purging Behavior (Bulimia): The capacity to consume food rapidly and then eliminate it permits the organism to benefit from the taste/reward without fully absorbing calories — a solution to environments of feast-famine oscillation. - Pica: Non-nutritive ingestion is a primitive micronutrientseeking behavior — the organism attempts to obtain minerals (iron, zinc) missing from the diet. Sovereignty Gate: The same thrifty metabolism that stored fat for winter now produces diabetes in a 24-hour grocery environment. The same appetite suppression that preserved resources during famine now produces cardiac arrest in a thin-ideal culture. The environment, not the phenotype, is the variable.

## 11 — Elimination Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Social demands for sphincter control before neurological maturation is complete, or traumatic toilet training. Phenotypic Advantage: - Nocturnal Enuresis: Vasopressin circadian rhythm that has not yet entrained to the light-dark cycle. In ancestral environments, nighttime urination would have been adaptive — it prevented bladder distension during sleep when predators might approach. Encopresis (Retentive): The capacity to withhold stool despite rectal fullness permits the child to maintain play/activity without interruption. The same neural circuit that produces constipation under stress in modern adults was protective in ancestral environments where defecation was a vulnerable position. Sovereignty Gate: These are maturational delays, not pathologies. The Entropy Threshold is social — it is the point at which peer rejection causes more damage than the elimination behavior itself.

## 12 — Sleep-Wake Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Environments requiring night vigilance (security, hunting, parenting) or shift-based activity patterns. Phenotypic Advantage: - Insomnia Phenotype: The capacity to remain awake when others sleep permits night guarding of the group. Individuals with the insomnia phenotype have a wider circadian window and can function in shift work, parenting, and security roles. - Delayed Phase: The evening chronotype permits night-time social bonding, hunting, and protection. In adolescent development, the phase delay is biologically programmed — it permits peer bonding during the evening hours when adult supervision is minimal. - Advanced Phase: The morning chronotype permits early-morning hunting, foraging, and group protection when predators are most active at dawn. - Restless Legs: The urge to move the legs may have prevented prolonged immobility during sleep in ancestral environments where sleeping on the ground required periodic position changes. Sovereignty Gate: The circadian diversity of a population ensures that someone is always awake to guard the group. The modern environment’s demand for synchronized schedules creates the pathology, not the variation itself.

## 13 — Sexual Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Social demands for sexual performance on schedule, partner compatibility mismatches, or reproductive pressure. Phenotypic Advantage: - Erectile/Vasocongestive Variability: The capacity to modulate arousal based on safety assessment. In ancestral environments, sexual function required a perception of safety — an erect penis or engorged clitoris in a dangerous environment would have been maladaptive. The “dysfunction” may be an accurate safety assessment. - Delayed Ejaculation:

Prolonged intercourse may increase the probability of conception through extended stimulation. In ancestral environments, stamina was selected for. - Premature Ejaculation: Rapid ejaculation permits copulation completion before threat arrives. In high-predation environments, speed of reproduction was selected for over duration. - Low Desire: The capacity to suppress sexual drive during stress, illness, or resource scarcity conserves energy for survival priorities. Sovereignty Gate: The same arousal circuit that functions perfectly in safety fails in performanceevaluation contexts. The sexual phenotype is not broken — the environmental context of scrutiny is mismatched to the neurological safety requirement.

## 14 — Gender Incongruence Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Binary sex-role environments that do not accommodate the natural variation in gender identity development. Phenotypic Advantage: - Gender Identity Variation: Neurodevelopmental variation in gender identity reflects the natural biological diversity of human sex development. The same prenatal hormone exposure patterns that produce gender diversity also produce cognitive diversity — enhanced spatial abilities, verbal fluency, or systemic processing depending on the specific neuroendocrine profile. - The capacity for self-awareness of gender incongruence requires a level of interoceptive and introspective capability that is itself a cognitive advantage. Sovereignty Gate: The distress is not inherent to the gender incongruence — it is generated by environmental non-accommodation. In environments that permit gender transition, the incongruence resolves and the individual’s cognitive profile remains intact.

## 15 — Disruptive, Impulse-Control, & Conduct Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Hierarchical or competitive environments where social dominance determines resource access, or threatening environments requiring preemptive aggression. Phenotypic Advantage: - Oppositional Reactivity: The capacity to resist authority when authority is unjust or incompetent. In ancestral environments, individuals who challenged dominant figures prevented group exploitation. In modern contexts, advantageous in whistleblowing, activism, and civil rights. - Conduct Dysregulation: The capacity for aggressive resource acquisition in competitive environments where cooperation is not reciprocated. The same risk-taking behavior that produces incarceration in one context produces entrepreneurship in another. - Empathy Circuit Hypofunction (Antisocial): The capacity to make decisions without emotional interference permits rapid, ruthless action in high-stakes environments where sentiment would be lethal (military leadership, crisis surgery, corporate restructuring). The fearlessness that characterizes this phenotype is a genuine advantage in combat. Sovereignty Gate: The same behavioral profile that produces a criminal in a deprived environment produces a CEO or military leader in an enriched one. The Entropy Threshold is crossed not by the behavior itself but by the environmental consequences.

## 16 — Substance-Related & Addictive Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Environments of chronic stress, trauma, or resource scarcity where endogenous reward signaling is insufficient to maintain motivation. Phenotypic Advantage: - Substance Use: Exogenous reward consumption is a universal primate behavior — every known human culture has used psychoactive substances. The dopamine-reward circuit that substance use exploits exists because it motivates approach to natural rewards (food, sex, social bonding). Substance use is not pathological reward seeking — it is an attempt to activate a reward circuit that the environment has failed to stimulate. - Withdrawal Phenotypes: The withdrawal syndrome represents the nervous system’s attempt to restore homeostasis after exogenous perturbation. The distress of withdrawal is the body’s signal that the substance has been providing necessary regulation. Sovereignty Gate: Substance use is an environmental adaptation to an environment that does not provide sufficient natural reward. The phenotype becomes somatic debt when the substance use itself damages the reward circuit (dopamine receptor downregulation) — the organism then needs the substance just to reach baseline, a state indistinguishable from needing antidepressants just to reach baseline.

## 17 — Neurocognitive Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Aging environments with accumulating cellular damage — the cost of living in an oxygen atmosphere. Phenotypic Advantage: - Mild Neurocognitive Phenotype: The gradual cognitive decline of aging permits the transfer of knowledge from older to younger generations. The wisdom accumulated over a lifetime compensates for processing speed decline. In ancestral environments, elders who could not hunt were valued for their knowledge of survival strategies. - Delirium: The acute confusional state is a sign of systemic metabolic crisis — it forces the organism (and the group) to attend to the underlying medical problem. Delirium is the brain’s smoke alarm. - Alzheimer’s Proteinopathy: The amyloid-beta aggregation may be an antimicrobial defense mechanism — the plaques trap pathogens. The trade-off is that the same mechanism that protects against brain infection produces neurodegeneration. Sovereignty Gate: Neurocognitive decline is not a disease — it is the predictable consequence of a system that works perfectly for decades but eventually accumulates damage. The Entropy Threshold is crossed when the damage exceeds the compensation capacity.

## 18 — Personality Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Social group living requiring a diversity of behavioral strategies within the population. Phenotypic Advantage: - Paranoid Threat-Calibration: In environments where deception and betrayal are common, the capacity to detect threat in social signals is protective. Paranoid individuals detect cheaters and prevent exploitation. The “paranoid” style in intelligence analysis is correct more often than naive trust. - Schizoid Detachment: The capacity to function without social reward permits independence of thought and action. In environments requiring solitary work (research, art, exploration), the schizoid temperament is advantageous. - Borderline Emotional Dysregulation: The heightened emotional sensitivity that produces interpersonal chaos also produces intense creativity, empathy, and passion. The capacity for rapid emotional shifts permits flexible responses to changing environments. - Narcissistic Self-Enhancement: The capacity for grandiosity permits risk-taking that others would avoid. In ancestral environments, narcissistic individuals led dangerous expeditions. In modern contexts, they drive innovation but also exploitation. - Obsessive-Compulsive Control: The need for order and precision produces reliable work output. In environments requiring error-free performance, the obsessive style is ideal. Sovereignty Gate: Personality “pathology” is the same neural architecture that produces behavioral diversity in the population. The same trait that makes one person a criminal makes another a CEO. The environment determines which expression occurs.

## 19 — Paraphilic Arousal Template Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: Restricted access to normative sexual outlets, or developmental exposure to atypical stimuli during critical periods of sexual template formation. Phenotypic Advantage: - Arousal Template Plasticity: The capacity to form sexual associations to novel stimuli is the same mechanism that permits sexual behavior to occur under diverse conditions. Without this plasticity, reproduction would be restricted to a narrow range of contexts. Paraphilic Variation: The same associative learning mechanism that produces normative attraction produces atypical attraction when the developmental environment is atypical. The plasticity is the advantage — the specific content is contingent on exposure. - Pedophilic Arousal: Note: No advantage is claimed for behavior that produces harm. The biological substrate of attraction to immature features may reflect ancestral reproductive strategies where early reproduction was advantageous in high-mortality environments. Sovereignty Gate: The arousal template itself is not the pathology — the harm caused to nonconsenting others is. The distinction between paraphilia (atypical arousal) and paraphilic disorder (harmful behavior) is the presence of harm.

## 20 — Other, Medication-Induced, & Conditions Phenotypes

Environmental Friction: The intersection of biological vulnerability with iatrogenic (treatmentinduced) or environmental stress. Phenotypic Advantage: - Medication-Induced Phenotypes: The same D2 receptor blockade that produces parkinsonism also treats psychosis. The side effect and the therapeutic effect are inseparable — both are consequences of the same biological manipulation. - Relational Stress Phenotypes: The capacity for attachment to caregivers who are neglectful or abusive ensures survival of the young even in suboptimal care environments. The attachment system’s priority is survival, not happiness. - Proposed/Research Phenotypes: These represent the frontier of our understanding — phenotypes that have been identified but whose biological mechanisms are still being mapped. Sovereignty Gate: The most fundamental principle: no phenotype exists without a reason. Every allostatic state is an attempt by the organism to survive its environment. The question is always: what is this organism trying to survive?

## Synthesis: The Unified Field Principle

Every phenotype in this archive follows the same logic: 1. An environmental pressure selects for a specific biological response 2. The response is the organism’s attempt to survive that pressure 3. The response becomes “pathological” when the pressure persists beyond the response’s useful window OR when the environment changes but the response does not 4. The response is a Protected Phenotype when it operates within the environment that selected for it 5. The response becomes Somatic Debt when it operates in an environment where it no longer provides advantage The Entropy Threshold is always the same: the point at which the cost of the adaptation exceeds the cost of the original environmental pressure. The clinical implication: do not suppress the adaptation. Change the environment, remove the pressure, support the organism’s capacity to adapt again.
