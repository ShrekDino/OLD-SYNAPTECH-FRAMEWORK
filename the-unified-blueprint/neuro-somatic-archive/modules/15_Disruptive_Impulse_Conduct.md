# Module 15 — Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Phenotypes

This module reclassifies disruptive, impulse-control, and conduct disorders as dysregulation of prefrontal-limbic circuitry governing threat detection, reward processing, aggression gating, and autonomic fear conditioning. Each phenotype reflects a characteristic pattern of autonomic hyporeactivity, serotonergic hypofunction, and structural prefrontal deficits.

### 15.1 Oppositional Defiant Phenotype (formerly Oppositional Defiant Disorder 313.81 [F91.3])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Dysregulation of prefrontal-limbic circuitry with chronically elevated amygdala reactivity and insufficient ventromedial PFC top-down inhibition, producing a sustained threat-detection bias. Hypocortisolemic baseline with blunted autonomic arousal permits norm-violating behavior without proportionate somatic signaling.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Loses temper | Amygdala hyperreactivity with PFC failure to gate aggressive impulse | Hyperactive right amygdala; hypoactive orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) |
| Touchy/easily annoyed | Limbic hypersensitivity with aberrant salience attribution to neutral stimuli | Elevated norepinephrine turnover; reduced serotonin transporter binding in anterior cingulate |
| Angry/resentful | Persistent cortisol dysregulation with inflammatory cytokine elevation | HPA axis blunting; low basal cortisol |
| Argues with authority | Prefrontal disinhibition of reactive aggression circuits | Reduced ventrolateral PFC volume; impaired fronto-amygdalar connectivity |
| Defies/refuses compliance | Striatal reward hypersensitivity to oppositional behavior | Exaggerated dopamine response in ventral striatum during defiant acts |
| Deliberately annoys others | Operant conditioning of negative reinforcement via social provocation | Serotonergic deficit in raphe nuclei projections to PFC |
| Blames others | Aberrant self-referential processing with dorsal mPFC over-rehearsal | Hyperactivation of posterior cingulate and medial PFC during self-attribution |
| Spiteful/vindictive | Threat-reward coupling where retaliation activates mesolimbic reward | Elevated dopamine release in nucleus accumbens during revenge planning |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Resting heart rate | Radial pulse, seated 5 min, 3 measurements | <65 bpm (age 8–12) indicates blunted autonomic fear response |
| Skin conductance reactivity | Palmar conductance measured during startle probe | <2 microsiemens change from baseline = reduced fear conditioning |
| Cortisol awakening response | Salivary cortisol at 0, 30, 45 min post-waking | Flat slope (<25% rise from T0 to T30) = HPA hyporeactivity |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | <2 symptoms; behaviors context-appropriate | Normal HR variability; cortisol awakening response intact |
| Transition Zone | 2–3 symptoms in single setting | Blunted HR; morning cortisol <15 nmol/L |
| Somatic Debt | >4 symptoms across >2 settings | HR <60 bpm; cortisol <10 nmol/L at waking; low skin conductance |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to accurate threat-calibration: the phenotype shall not be assigned without objective autonomic measurement.
2. Right to circadian integrity: no diagnosis shall be rendered without HPA axis profiling.
3. Right to developmental context: allostatic thresholds must be age-normed; preschool patterns are not adult pathology.
4. Right to forensic corroboration: self-report alone is insufficient; at least one somatic proxy must be documented.

### 15.2 Intermittent Explosive Phenotype (formerly Intermittent Explosive Disorder 312.34 [F63.81])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Serotonergic hypofunction in prefrontal-limbic circuitry, specifically reduced ligand binding in anterior cingulate cortex and OFC, paired with amygdala hyperresponsiveness to anger-eliciting stimuli. Aggressive outbursts represent a failure of prefrontal brake mechanisms over primitive brainstem fight-flight programs.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Verbal aggression twice weekly for 3 months | Low threshold for amygdala-driven verbal outburst with insufficient ventromedial PFC dampening | Reduced serotonin transporter density in ACC; amygdala hyperactivation on fMRI |
| Physical aggression damaging property/people 3x/year | Brainstem periaqueductal gray recruitment without cortical override | Elevated CSF glutamate; low CSF 5-HIAA |
| Outburst disproportionate to provocation | Aberrant salience network assigning threat value to neutral stimuli | Hyperconnectivity between amygdala and insula; reduced fronto-limbic coupling |
| Outbursts impulsive, not premeditated | Striatal-cortical loop disconnection; habitual aggression circuits active | Reduced OFC gray matter volume; low monoamine oxidase A activity |
| Distress/impairment | Cumulative allostatic cost of repeated sympathetic surges | Elevated inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6); nocturnal BP non-dipping |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Pupillary response to anger faces | Infrared pupillometry during angry-face stimulus presentation | >30% dilation increase from baseline indicates amygdala hyperreactivity |
| Serotonergic probe (fenfluramine challenge) | Prolactin response to 60 mg fenfluramine orally | Peak prolactin <15 ng/mL = blunted serotonergic response |
| Startle eyeblink magnitude | EMG of orbicularis oculi during acoustic startle with prepulse | Prepulse inhibition <20% = deficient sensorimotor gating |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No aggressive outbursts | Normal amygdala-PFC connectivity; serotonin function intact |
| Transition Zone | Verbal outbursts only, <2x/week | Mildly elevated anger-face pupil response; intermittent sleep disruption |
| Somatic Debt | Physical aggression with property damage | Low 5-HIAA in CSF; elevated IL-6; structural OFC deficits |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to serotonergic profiling: no phenotype assignment without assessment of central serotonin function.
2. The right to state-context separation: intoxication or withdrawal must be ruled out via toxicology.
3. The right to developmental staging: no diagnosis before chronological age 6 years or equivalent developmental level.
4. The right to neuroimaging corroboration in forensic settings: structural OFC integrity must be documented.

### 15.3 Conduct Phenotype (formerly Conduct Disorder 312.81–312.89 [F91.1–F91.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Multisystem dysregulation: reduced autonomic fear conditioning (low resting heart rate, low skin conductance), frontotemporal-limbic structural deficits (reduced ventral PFC and amygdala volumes), and heightened threat-misperception in ambiguous social contexts. The phenotype represents an organism adapted to high-threat environments via proactive aggression and emotional blunting.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Bullies/threatens/intimidates | Instrumental aggression mediated by ventral striatal reward anticipation | Elevated dopamine release in nucleus accumbens during dominance encounters |
| Initiates physical fights | Reactive aggression with deficient PFC inhibitory control | Reduced PFC gray matter; low resting heart rate |
| Used weapon | Prefrontal disinhibition permitting lethal force recruitment | OFC volume <10th percentile for age |
| Physically cruel to people | Empathy-circuit hypofunction: absent mirror neuron activation | Reduced activity in anterior insula and ACC during pain observation |
| Physically cruel to animals | Callous trait expression; amygdala hyporesponsive to distress cues | Reduced amygdala activation to fearful faces; low skin conductance response |
| Stolen with victim confrontation | Reward seeking without fear of consequence | Low baseline cortisol; blunted autonomic reactivity |
| Forced sexual activity | Aggression-reward coupling with absent empathic inhibition | Hyperactivation of ventral striatum; reduced vmPFC-amygdala connectivity |
| Fire-setting | Novelty/reward seeking with destructive outcome indifference | High sensation-seeking physiology; low monoamine oxidase |
| Destroyed property | Impulse control circuit failure | Reduced OFC volume; frontal lobe white matter microstructural abnormalities |
| Broken into building/car | Instrumental reward seeking with reduced punishment sensitivity | Low resting HR; reduced skin conductance during anticipation of consequences |
| Lies/con men | Verbal manipulation as adaptive strategy in threat-dense environment | Elevated cortisol reactivity during deception; PFC hyperactivation for cognitive load |
| Stolen without confrontation | Procedural reward seeking with intact risk calculation | Normal autonomic function; striatal dopamine dysregulation |
| Stays out at night despite prohibition | Circadian dysregulation coupled with autonomy-seeking | Delayed dim-light melatonin onset; reduced sleep pressure |
| Ran away from home | Escape response to hostile environment; HPA axis hyperreactivity | Elevated baseline cortisol in home environment; flattened diurnal slope |
| Truant from school | Avoidance of low-reward, high-threat social environment | Low dopamine receptor availability in striatum; school-context HR elevation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Resting heart rate (seated) | 5-min seated, radial pulse, 3 measurements averaged | <60 bpm (age 10–14) = reduced autonomic fear response |
| Skin conductance during stressor | Palmar conductance during video of peer conflict | <1 microsiemens change = deficient emotional arousal |
| Fear-potentiated startle | Eyeblink EMG during threat-cued startle paradigm | Absent fear potentiation = callous-unemotional trait marker |
| Urinary cortisol (overnight) | Overnight urine collection normalized to creatinine | <10 μg/g creatinine = HPA hyporeactivity |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | <3 symptoms, no aggression | Normal HR, HRV, cortisol; intact fear-potentiated startle |
| Transition Zone | 3–5 symptoms, aggression absent or mild | HR 60–70 bpm; attenuated cortisol response |
| Somatic Debt | >5 symptoms with cruelty or weapon use | HR <60 bpm; absent fear-potentiated startle; flat cortisol slope |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to autonomic baseline recording: conduct phenotype shall not be adjudicated without HR and skin conductance data.
2. The right to environmental context analysis: trauma history and threat exposure must be mapped before allostatic interpretation.
3. The right to age-appropriate normative calibration: behavior must exceed developmental norms, not merely be non-compliant.
4. The right to callous-unemotional trait differentiation: the "with limited prosocial emotions" specifier requires biometric confirmation of empathy-circuit hypofunction.

### 15.4 Antisocial Phenotype (formerly Antisocial Personality Disorder 301.7 [F60.2])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Convergent autonomic (low resting HR, reduced skin conductance), neurostructural (reduced prefrontal gray matter, amygdala volume reduction), and neurochemical (low serotonin turnover, elevated testosterone-cortisol ratio) aberrations producing a phenotype optimized for exploitative dominance in zero-sum social environments.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Failure to conform to social norms | Prefrontal regulatory failure with striatal reward dominance | Reduced PFC volume; low MAO-A activity |
| Deceitfulness, repeated lying | Instrumental verbal behavior without emotional valence | Reduced amygdala activation during deception |
| Impulsivity/failure to plan | Orbitofrontal-striatal disconnect impairing future consequence encoding | Reduced OFC-amygdala functional connectivity |
| Irritability/aggressiveness | Low threshold for brainstem threat-response recruitment | Hyperactive amygdala; low serotonin transporter binding |
| Reckless disregard for safety | Absent fear conditioning to danger cues | Low resting HR; absent skin conductance response to aversive stimuli |
| Consistent irresponsibility | Reward discounting of future obligations | Elevated delay discounting on neurocognitive tasks |
| Lack of remorse | Empathic processing circuit failure | Reduced anterior insula and ACC activity during pain observation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Resting heart rate (seated, 5 min) | Radial or carotid pulse, average of 3 readings | <65 bpm (adult male); <70 bpm (adult female) |
| Skin conductance orienting response | Palmar conductance to novel tones (15 tones, 70 dB) | <3 orienting responses across 15 trials = reduced autonomic reactivity |
| Testosterone/cortisol ratio | Serum sampling at 0800h | Testosterone:cortisol ratio >0.05 (nmol/L:nmol/L) |
| Startle potentiation to distress cues | EMG eyeblink during viewing of crying infant or victim distress | Absent startle augmentation = empathy-circuit hypofunction |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No antisocial conduct after age 18 | Normal HR and skin conductance; intact startle potentiation |
| Transition Zone | Some rule-breaking but no physical cruelty | HR 65–72 bpm; moderate autonomic blunting |
| Somatic Debt | Full criteria with callous remorselessness | HR <65 bpm; absent startle; T:C ratio >0.05 |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to conduct-history verification: antisocial phenotype requires documented conduct phenotype before age 15.
2. The right to biometric independence: diagnosis shall not rest on self-report alone; minimum two autonomic proxies.
3. The right to substance-ruleout: antisocial behavior exclusively during intoxication does not constitute the phenotype.
4. The right to neurodevelopmental context: traumatic brain injury and prenatal toxin exposure must be excluded.

### 15.5 Pyromania Phenotype (formerly Pyromania 312.33 [F63.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Affect-regulation circuitry dysfunction in which fire-setting serves as an autonomic stimulant to raise arousal from a hypoactive baseline. The fire-setting act triggers sympathetic activation that is subjectively experienced as pleasurable, reinforced by dopamine release in ventral striatum.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Deliberate fire-setting on multiple occasions | Operant conditioning of fire-stimulus reward coupling | Dopamine release in nucleus accumbens during fire ignition |
| Tension/arousal before the act | Anticipatory sympathetic recruitment from hypoactive baseline | Rising HR, skin conductance before fire-setting; low baseline autonomic tone |
| Fascination with fire | Reward-salience attribution to fire-related stimuli | Hyperactivation of ventral striatum and ACC when viewing fire images |
| Pleasure/gratification/relief when setting fires | Sympathetic activation reward; dopamine-mediated relief | Elevated HR during act; post-act dopamine surge |
| Not for monetary/ideological/anger motive | Distinct from instrumental aggression; pure reward-seeking | Reward circuitry activation without PFC instrumental planning |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Heart rate during fire imagery | Continuous HR monitoring during fire image presentation | HR increase >15 bpm from baseline to fire images |
| Skin conductance level (baseline) | Palmar conductance, 5-min resting baseline | <3 microsiemens = hypoarousal baseline |
| Pupil dilation to fire videos | Infrared pupillometry during fire-setting video | >25% dilation increase indicates aberrant salience |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No fire-setting interest | Normal autonomic response to fire stimuli |
| Transition Zone | Fascination but no fire-setting acts | Elevated HR response to fire; baseline hypoarousal |
| Somatic Debt | Multiple fire-setting episodes with pleasure | Baseline HR <65 with >20 bpm increase during fire; dopamine reward signaling |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to motive differentiation: instrumental fire-setting (fraud, revenge, concealment) excludes the phenotype.
2. The right to conduct-disorder exclusion: fire-setting embedded in broader conduct pattern is not pyromania.
3. The right to autonomic profiling: baseline hypoarousal must be documented before phenotype assignment.
4. The right to intoxication ruleout: fire-setting during substance intoxication is not pyromania.

### 15.6 Kleptomania Phenotype (formerly Kleptomania 312.32 [F63.2])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Dysregulation of reward, impulse control, and affect-regulation circuits. Stealing activates a dopamine-mediated reward cycle: rising tension (hypo-dopaminergic baseline) → theft (dopamine surge) → relief/pleasure (opioid-mediated). The act is compulsive, not instrumental.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Recurrent failure to resist impulses to steal | Striatal-cortical loop dysfunction; impaired response inhibition | Reduced prefrontal inhibitory control; ventral striatal hyperactivation |
| Rising tension before theft | Dopamine craving state; anticipatory anxiety | Low baseline dopamine; heightened noradrenergic tone pre-theft |
| Pleasure/gratification/relief at time of theft | Dopamine surge with opioid-mediated reward | Elevated dopamine and endogenous opioids during theft |
| Objects not needed for personal/monetary value | Reward dissociation from utility; act itself is reinforcer | Dopamine release independent of object value |
| Not for anger/vengeance | Distinct from instrumental theft | Reward circuitry activation without emotional motivation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Skin conductance during theft imagery | Palmar conductance while imagining stealing scenario | >50% increase pre-theft imagery; rapid drop post-act |
| Pupillary response to forbidden objects | Pupillometry during viewing of desirable but unowned objects | >20% dilation = reward anticipation |
| Response inhibition task (Go/No-Go) | Computerized Go/No-Go with theft-related images | >30% commission errors on theft-cued No-Go trials |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No stealing impulses | Normal Go/No-Go performance; intact impulse control |
| Transition Zone | Occasional theft fantasy, no acting out | Elevated pre-act tension on self-report; mild commission errors |
| Somatic Debt | Recurrent theft with tension-relief cycle | High commission errors; autonomic hyperreactivity pre-theft |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to motive exclusion: theft for instrumental gain (value, rebellion, dare) is not the phenotype.
2. The right to impulse-control verification: stealing must be ego-dystonic (attempted resistance) and not part of broader antisocial pattern.
3. The right to neurocognitive profiling: response inhibition must be assessed via Go/No-Go.
4. The right to forensic caution: malingering must be ruled out in criminal justice contexts.

### 15.7 Other Specified Disruptive Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Disorder 312.89 [F91.8])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Subthreshold dysregulation across one or more of the above domains. The presentation causes allostatic cost without meeting full criteria for any specific phenotype.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of disruptive/impulse-control/conduct class | Partial-circuit recruitment without full-system dysregulation | Intermediate autonomic/neuroimaging findings |
| Clinically significant distress/impairment | Allostatic load accumulating without syndrome-specific pattern | Elevated CRP, IL-6; sleep disruption; circadian misalignment |
| Does not meet full criteria for any specific disorder | Threshold not reached for categorical classification | Autonomic and neurochemical values in transition zone |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Composite autonomic battery | HR + skin conductance + cortisol | At least one measure in transition zone |
| Impulse control screen | Go/No-Go commission errors | >15% commission errors |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No impairing symptoms | All measures normal |
| Transition Zone | Some symptoms, impairment present | 1–2 autonomic measures in transition zone |
| Somatic Debt | Subthreshold across multiple domains | Multiple markers approaching categorical thresholds |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to specified documentation: the specific reason for subthreshold presentation must be recorded.
2. The right to allostatic monitoring: biomarkers should track progression toward or remission from full phenotype.

### 15.8 Unspecified Disruptive Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Disorder 312.9 [F91.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Insufficient information exists to map the allostatic architecture. Reserved for emergency or limited-data contexts.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of class | Unknown circuit state | Data insufficient |
| Clinically significant distress/impairment | Presumed allostatic load | Not yet measured |
| Information insufficient for specific diagnosis | Assessment gap | No biomarkers available |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| N/A | Assessment deferred | N/A |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Indeterminate | Insufficient data | Not assessed |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to timely assessment: unspecified designation is temporary; full evaluation shall follow within 72 hours.
2. The right to provisional status: unspecified phenotype carries no forensic weight until biomarked.
