# Module 06 — Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Adaptations

Phenotypes from the Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6), reclassifying DSM-5 Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Adaptations disorders through a neuro-somatic lens.

### Obsessive-Compulsive Phenotype (formerly Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder 300.3 [F42])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) loop hyperactivation with orbitofrontal-anterior cingulatestriatal overdrive; caudate nucleus failure to gate intrusive corticothalamic information; striatal dopamine D2 receptor dysregulation; error-detection hyperreactivity in anterior cingulate; serotonin transporter polymorphism affecting 5-HT tone in CSTC circuitry.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Recurrent intrusive thoughts, urges, images | CSTC loop gating failure; orbitofrontal cortex overactivity driving intrusive content | Orbitofrontal cortex, caudate nucleus (head), anterior cingulate cortex |
| A2: Compulsions to neutralize obsessions | Striatal procedural habit-system recruitment as anxiety-reduction strategy | Putamen, globus pallidus, thalamus |
| B: Time-consuming ( >1hr/ day) or clinically significant | CSTC hyperactivation reaching metabolic threshold for functional interference | Increased glucose metabolism in OFC-caudate-ACC circuit on PET |
| Tic-related specifier dysregulation; cortical-striatal-thalamic | Overlap with basal ganglia dopamine motor area | Caudate, putamen, supplementary development variant |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Glabellar tap response | Percussion of glabella while eyes closed; count of reflex blinks before extinction | >5 blinks before extinction suggests striatal gating deficit |
| Oculomotor saccade latency | Saccade speed during anti-saccade task (look opposite direction of stimulus) | Increased latency >150ms suggests frontal-striatal inhibition failure |
| Corneal reflex hyperreactivity | Light corneal touch with cotton wisp; blink amplitude | Blink amplitude >2/4 on reflex scale; bilateral over-response |
| Fine motor persistence | Finger-to-thumb sequential tapping speed over 30s | Speed decay >15% over 30s suggests striatal procedural fatigue |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Occasional intrusive thoughts without compulsion; no functional interference | Normal CSTC metabolic activity; striatal gating intact |
| Transition Zone | Subclinical obsessions/compulsions spending <1hr/day; good insight | Mild OFC-striatal hypermetabolism; able to suppress with effort |
| Somatic Debt consuming compulsions; poor or absent | Debilitating obsessions with time- volume reduction; comorbid tic disorder | Marked CSTC hypermetabolism on PET; striatal insight |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to striatal gating of intrusive corticothalamic information without procedural habit recruitment
2. Right to error-detection signaling proportionate to actual threat, not anterior cingulate hyperreactivity
3. Right to behavioral completion free of caudate-driven incompleteness signaling
4. Right to cognitive flexibility uncompromised by orbitofrontal perseveration

### Possession Retention Phenotype (formerly Hoarding Disorder 300.3 [F42])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Anterior cingulate and insular hyperreactivity to discarding decisions (anticipatory loss distress); ventromedial prefrontal valuation system dysfunction assigning excessive salience to saved objects; parietal-insula object-self boundary overlap; prefrontal executive planning and categorization deficits.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|

| C: Cluttered living areas compromising use | Executive planning deficit; prefrontal categorization/organization failure | Dorsolateral PFC, anterior cingulate |
| With excessive acquisition specifier | Striatal reward-system recruitment for acquisition behavior | Nucleus accumbens, caudate |

### Hair-Pulling Phenotype (formerly Trichotillomania / Hair-Pulling Disorder 312.39 [F63.3])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Sensorimotor striatal loop hyperactivation for hair-directed procedural behavior; somatosensory cortex hypersensitivity to hair-texture signals; opioidergic reward dysregulation providing tensionrelief reinforcement; prefrontal impulse-control under-recruitment during behavioral execution.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|

| Associated: Tension preceding pulling; relief | Opioidergic tension-release reward; sensory craving-satisfaction cycle | Periaqueductal gray opioid receptors, somatosensory cortex |

### Unspecified OC & Related Presentation (formerly Unspecified OCD & Related Disorder 300.3 [F42])

**Allostatic Load Profile**
