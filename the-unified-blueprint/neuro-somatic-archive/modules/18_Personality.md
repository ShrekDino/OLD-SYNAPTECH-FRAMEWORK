# Module 18: Personality Phenotypes

This module covers personality phenotypes, reclassified from DSM-5 Personality Disorders. Each phenotype is re-interpreted through the lens of underlying neurocircuitry dysregulation, allostatic load profiles, and somatic markers rather than behavioral criteria alone.

### Paranoid Personality Phenotype (formerly Paranoid Personality Disorder 301.0 [F60.0])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Chronic hypervigilance mediated by sustained amygdala-hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis activation. Elevated baseline norepinephrine and cortisol create a persistent threat-detection state. Striatal dopamine dysregulation contributes to aberrant salience attribution to neutral social stimuli. Default mode network hypoconnectivity with amygdala hyperreactivity produces impaired social safety learning.

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Hypoactive social reward circuitry with reduced ventral striatum dopamine response to social stimuli. Low baseline sympathetic tone with blunted autonomic reactivity to interpersonal engagement. **Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to comprehensive neuro-somatic evaluation before definitive phenotyping
2. Right to provisional supportive intervention while awaiting biological characterization
3. Right to freedom from premature diagnostic labeling without biological basis
4. Right to thorough history-taking to distinguish phenotype from medical/substance causes

### Personality Change Due to Another Medical Condition (formerly 310.1 [F07.0])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Acquired neuro-somatic reorganization secondary to demonstrable CNS pathology. The personality change is a direct consequence of structural or functional brain insult — not an adaptive or developmental phenomenon. Specific profile depends on lesion localization: frontal lobe → disinhibition; temporal lobe → affective lability; subcortical → apathy.
