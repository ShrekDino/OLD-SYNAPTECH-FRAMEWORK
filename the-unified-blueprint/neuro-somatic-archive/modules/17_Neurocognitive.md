# Module 17: Neurocognitive Phenotypes

This module covers neurocognitive phenotypes, reclassified from DSM-5 Neurocognitive Disorders (Delirium, Major and Mild NCDs). Each phenotype is re-interpreted through neurodegenerative, vascular, inflammatory, or metabolic mechanisms producing measurable cognitive decline.

### Delirium (formerly Delirium 293.0 [F05])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Acute neuroinflammatory-metabolic decompensation. Primary mechanism: failure of cholinergicdopaminergic homeostatic regulation secondary to systemic illness, intoxication, withdrawal, or polypharmacy. Etiological subtypes: substance intoxication, substance withdrawal, medicationinduced, medical condition, multiple etiologies.

### Major Neurocognitive Phenotype — Alzheimer’s Disease (formerly Major NCD due to Alzheimer’s Disease 294.1x [F02.8x])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Protein misfolding cascade with amyloid-beta oligomer accumulation and tau hyperphosphorylation. Primary mechanism: failure of proteostatic clearance → synaptic loss → neuronal death beginning in entorhinal cortex and hippocampus. Etiological subtype: sporadic (APOE4 risk) vs. autosomal dominant (APP, PSEN1, PSEN2).

### Major Neurocognitive Phenotype — Traumatic Brain Injury (formerly Major NCD due to TBI 294.1x [F02.8x])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Focal or diffuse axonal injury from biomechanical force transmission through brain parenchyma. ### Major Neurocognitive Phenotype — Huntington’s Disease (formerly Major NCD due to Huntington’s Disease 294.1x [F02.8x])
