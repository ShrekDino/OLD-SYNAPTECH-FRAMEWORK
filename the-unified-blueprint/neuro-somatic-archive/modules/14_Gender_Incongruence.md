# Module 14 — Gender Incongruence Phenotypes

This module reclassifies gender dysphoria as a neuro-somatic configuration mismatch between the central gender identity map (sexually dimorphic brain nuclei) and somatic sex characteristics. The allostatic load arises from chronic interoceptive conflict between the predicted body schema and actual somatic structure, mediated by HPA axis dysregulation and insular-cingulate alarm circuitry.

### 14.1 Gender Incongruence Phenotype — Childhood (formerly Gender Dysphoria in Children 302.6 [F64.2])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Prenatal androgen milieu atypical for the assigned gender creates a sex-dimorphic brain organization (bed nucleus of the stria terminalis, hypothalamic sexually dimorphic nucleus, interstitial nucleus of the anterior hypothalamus) that diverges from somatic sex development. This central-peripheral sex configuration mismatch produces chronic interoceptive conflict between the predicted body schema (cortical somatosensory homunculus representation) and actual somatic structure. Genetic contributions include polymorphisms in sex-steroid receptor genes (AR, ERα, ERβ, CYP19A1, SRD5A2) and early neuroendocrine signaling pathways.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Strong desire to be other gender or insistence one is other gender | Central gender identity map mismatch with somatic sex assignment | Sexually dimorphic brain nucleus configuration discordant with gonadal sex |
| A2: Cross-dressing preference (boys) / resistance to feminine attire (girls) | Behavioral alignment with central gender map | Sensory-affective drive to match body presentation to internal body schema |
| A3: Cross-gender roles in make-believe play | Identity-consistent behavioral rehearsal | Ventromedial prefrontal cortex gender-typical behavioral templates |
| A4: Preference for toys/games of other gender | Sex-typical play circuit activation patterns | Organizational effects of prenatal androgens on the corticostriatal play circuit |
| A5: Preference for playmates of other gender | Peer affiliation driven by shared behavioral phenotype | Oxytocinergic and vasopressinergic social affiliation circuit tuning |
| A6: Rejection of natal-sex-typical toys/activities | Aversive response to gender-discordant stimuli | Anterior cingulate and insula activation during gender-mismatched play |
| A7: Strong dislike of one's sexual anatomy | Interoceptive body schema violation | Parietal operculum somatosensory representation mismatch with genital structure |
| A8: Desire for sex characteristics of experienced gender | Anticipated somatic congruence drive | Motor cortex and body schema network projection to desired phenotype |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| 2D:4D digit ratio | Vernier caliper measurement of index-to-ring finger length | <0.95 (natal female) or >1.0 (natal male) indicates atypical prenatal androgen exposure |
| Anogenital distance | Caliper from anus to genital tubercle base | Below 25th percentile for assigned sex at birth |
| Serum AMH (anti-Müllerian hormone) prepubertal | Venipuncture, ELISA | Discordant with assigned sex reference range |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Gender-typical play and body comfort within assigned-sex range | Digit ratio sex-consistent; anogenital distance within assigned-sex range |
| Transition Zone | Cross-gender behavior present without anatomic dysphoria | Borderline digit ratio; behavioral cross-gender preference only |
| Somatic Debt | Full criteria including anatomic dysphoria for 6+ months; distress in social/school function | Digit ratio sex-atypical; anogenital distance sex-atypical; GH/IGF-1 discordant with assigned-sex trajectory |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The sexually dimorphic brain has the right to a gender identity map that matches its organizational configuration, regardless of somatic sex assignment.
2. The prepubertal body has the right to develop without forced alignment to social gender expectations that conflict with the central gender map.
3. The developing child has the right to pubertal blockade to prevent the development of secondary sex characteristics that would increase central-peripheral sex configuration mismatch.
4. The body schema network (parietal operculum, premotor cortex) has the right to congruence between predicted and actual somatic structure.

### 14.2 Gender Incongruence Phenotype — Adolescent and Adult (formerly Gender Dysphoria in Adolescents and Adults 302.85 [F64.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Persistent mismatch between the central gender identity map (sexually dimorphic brain nuclei: BNSTc, INAH3, SDN-POA) and the somatic sex characteristics present at puberty or in adulthood. The discrepancy produces chronic allostatic load mediated by the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis and anterior insula — anterior cingulate interoceptive alarm circuitry. Secondary sex characteristics that develop during puberty amplify the mismatch by creating irreversible somatic features (voice deepening, breast development, Adam's apple, facial hair, hip width) that conflict with the central body schema.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| A1: Incongruence between experienced gender and primary/secondary sex characteristics | Central gender map versus actual body configuration conflict | BNSTc and INAH3 volume and neuron count discordant with somatic sex |
| A2: Strong desire to be rid of primary/secondary sex characteristics | Homeostatic drive to eliminate mismatch-generating tissue | Parietal operculum body schema signals "foreign" status of natal sex characteristics |
| A3: Strong desire for sex characteristics of other gender | Somatic completion drive | Motor cortex template for desired body form; mirror neuron system projection |
| A4: Strong desire to be of other gender | Identity-level resolution of central-peripheral mismatch | Default mode network self-representation map |
| A5: Strong desire to be treated as other gender | Social recognition need for identity validation | Temporoparietal junction social category processing; ventral striatum social reward |
| A6: Strong conviction of having typical feelings/reactions of other gender | Affective and autonomic response pattern matching central gender map | Amygdala — hypothalamus sex-typical stress response and emotional processing |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Salivary cortisol diurnal slope | Saliva samples at waking, 30 min post-waking, noon, bedtime | Flattened slope (loss of morning peak) indicates chronic HPA allostatic load |
| Heart rate variability (HRV) | 5-minute supine ECG; RMSSD analysis | RMSSD <25 ms indicates elevated sympathetic/parasympathetic imbalance |
| Serum brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) | Venipuncture, ELISA | <20 ng/mL associated with chronic stress from identity-somatic mismatch |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | Experienced gender congruent with assigned gender or fully transitioned with no distress | Normal diurnal cortisol slope; HRV >35 ms; BDNF >25 ng/mL |
| Transition Zone | Incongruence present but managed without significant HPA dysregulation | Mildly flattened cortisol slope; HRV 25–35 ms; BDNF 20–25 ng/mL |
| Somatic Debt | Marked incongruence with natal sex characteristics; significant distress; 6+ months duration | Flattened cortisol slope; HRV <25 ms; BDNF <20 ng/mL |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The central gender identity map (BNSTc, INAH3, SDN-POA) has the right to somatic congruence through hormonal and surgical alignment without being pathologized for its configuration.
2. The body schema network has the right to freedom from chronic interoceptive alarm caused by central-peripheral sex configuration mismatch.
3. The adolescent has the right to pubertal suppression to prevent irreversible somatic changes that would deepen the mismatch between the central gender map and body form.
4. The adult has the right to cross-sex hormone therapy and gender reassignment surgery as somatic corrective procedures for a documented neuroanatomical sex configuration discordant with assigned sex.

### 14.3 Other Specified Gender Incongruence Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Gender Dysphoria 302.6 [F64.8])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Central gender map mismatch with assigned sex that produces distress but does not meet the full duration or symptom threshold criteria. The neurobiological substrate is identical to the threshold phenotype — sexually dimorphic brain nucleus configuration discordant with somatic sex — but the allostatic load has not yet reached the categorical distress threshold, or the presentation is limited in duration.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of gender dysphoria not meeting full criteria | Partial or temporary central-peripheral sex configuration mismatch | BNSTc/INAH3 discordance of subthreshold intensity or duration |
| Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive alarm from body schema mismatch | Anterior insula — ACC activation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | Clinical interview verification | <6 months |
| Symptom count | Structured diagnostic interview assessment | <6 criteria (children) or <2 criteria (adolescents/adults) |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No gender-related distress | No mismatch indicators |
| Transition Zone | Subthreshold mismatch for <6 months | Partial biomarker elevation |
| Somatic Debt | Distressing mismatch below full threshold | Partial BNSTc discordance inferred; premorbid HPA dysregulation |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The individual has the right to clinical recognition of gender incongruence even when symptom duration has not reached 6 months.
2. The central gender identity map has the right to be assessed on its intrinsic configuration, not on the duration of distress it causes.
3. Subthreshold presentations have the right to the same neurobiological investigation as threshold presentations.
4. The individual has the right to somatic intervention based on documented mismatch regardless of whether arbitrary temporal thresholds are met.

### 14.4 Unspecified Gender Incongruence Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Gender Dysphoria 302.6 [F64.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Gender incongruence of unspecified origin where insufficient data exist to confirm the central gender map configuration. The absence of mapped neuroanatomical evidence does not indicate the absence of a central-peripheral sex configuration mismatch — only that the substrate has not been localized through available assessment methods.

**Somatic Architecture Table**

| DSM-5 Criterion | Allostatic Interpretation | Somatic Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Symptoms characteristic of gender dysphoria not meeting full criteria | Unmapped central-peripheral configuration discordance | Unknown pending neuroendocrine assessment |
| Clinically significant distress | Interoceptive detection of unmapped identity-somatic mismatch | Anterior insula — ACC activation |

**Forensic Proxy Markers**

| Proxy | Palpation/Measurement Protocol | Pathological Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive endocrine and neuroanatomical workup pending | Panel: karyotype, sex steroid levels, GnRH stimulation test, brain MRI | Pending results |

**Entropy Threshold**

| State | Criteria | Biological Markers |
|---|---|---|
| Protected Phenotype | No gender-related distress | All biomarkers within assigned-sex range |
| Somatic Debt | Distressing incongruence without identified mechanism | Biomarker workup incomplete or inconclusive |

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The individual has the right to clinical recognition of gender incongruence distress even when the precise neuroanatomical configuration has not been mapped.
2. The absence of a demonstrated BNSTc or INAH3 discordance shall not be grounds for dismissing the biological reality of the incongruence.
3. The individual has the right to comprehensive neuroendocrine investigation rather than arbitrary categorical withholding of care.
4. The individual has the right to somatic intervention (hormone therapy, surgery) based on documented distress and informed consent, regardless of whether central nervous system imaging confirms the mismatch.
