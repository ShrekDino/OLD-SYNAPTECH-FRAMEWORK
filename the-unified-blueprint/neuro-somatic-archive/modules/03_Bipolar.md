# Module 03 — Bipolar and Related Phenotypes

Bipolar phenotypes are reconceptualized as oscillatory allostatic states driven by catecholaminergic dysregulation and hypothalamic–pituitary–thyroid (HPT) axis disinhibition. These entries describe the autonomic, neuroendocrine, and circadian architecture of mood oscillations.

### Manic Activation Phenotype (formerly Manic Episode [296.40–

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A sympathoreticular hyperactivation state driven by catecholaminergic dysregulation and hypothalamic–pituitary–thyroid (HPT) axis disinhibition. Reduced cortical GABAergic gating of thalamocortical relay circuits produces the characteristic entropy acceleration. Sleep–wake cycle collapse reflects failed melatoninergic braking of the orexinergic arousal system. Positive allostatic load accumulates via sustained glucocorticoid receptor activation in prefrontal cortex, producing progressive dendritic retraction with each episode.

### Hypomanic Activation Phenotype (formerly Hypomanic Episode

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A subthreshold sympathoreticular activation state defined by preserved inhibitory brake capacity. Corticostriatal dopamine release is elevated relative to euthymic baseline but remains within the dynamic range of prefrontal regulatory control. Sleep architecture shows partial preservation: REM latency is shortened but not abolished. The distinguishing feature from the Manic Activation Phenotype is intact RSA modulation, indicating retained parasympathetic reserve.

### Depressive Collapse Phenotype (formerly Major Depressive Episode

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A glucocorticoid-mediated energy conservation state driven by sustained HPA axis hyperactivation with eventual adrenal exhaustion. Corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH) hypersecretion from the paraventricular nucleus produces downregulation of hippocampal glucocorticoid receptors, impairing negative feedback. The resulting hypercortisolemia suppresses monoamine synthesis via tryptophan–kynurenine pathway shunting. 5-HT1A autoreceptor upregulation in the raphe nucleus reduces serotonergic tone. Pro-inflammatory cytokine elevation (IL-6, TNF-α) induces sicknessbehavior circuits in the insula and anterior cingulate.

### Bipolar II Phenotype (formerly Bipolar II Disorder)

**Allostatic Load Profile**

An oscillatory allostatic system characterized by alternating Hypomanic Activation and Depressive Collapse phenotypes, with the hypomanic pole operating at sub-threshold autonomic intensity. The dominant time constant favors depressive polarity: approximately 3:1 ratio of weeks in depressive vs. hypomanic states. Striatal dopamine transporter (DAT) density is intermediate between bipolar I and unipolar populations, producing the characteristic asymmetry of preserved but fragile compensations. Dorsal raphe nucleus 5-HT1A binding potential is elevated, indicating tonic serotonergic inhibition of affective upswing.

### Cyclothymic Oscillation Phenotype (formerly Cyclothymic Disorder

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A low-amplitude, high-frequency mood oscillation system operating within the subthreshold autonomic range for at least 2 years. The oscillator’s time constant is approximately 7–14 days per half-cycle. Neither pole crosses the threshold for episode-level activation or collapse. The basal forebrain cholinergic system oscillates between states of relative hyper- and hypoactivity, producing subclinical shifts in arousal, appetite, and hedonic tone. The system is characterized by its chronicity rather than severity: the cumulative entropy burden accrues from the number of transitions rather than the depth of any single state.

### Substance/Medication-Induced Bipolar Phenotype (formerly

**Allostatic Load Profile**

An exogenously driven catecholaminergic hyperactivation state that mirrors the Manic Activation Phenotype but with a defined pharmacological initiator. The key distinguishing feature is the temporal coupling between substance exposure and autonomic shift, and the expected decay of activation upon substance elimination. The biological half-life of the inciting agent determines the duration envelope. Unlike the intrinsic Manic phenotype, the allostatic load here is primarily pharmacokinetic rather than neurodevelopmental: the system normalizes once the exogenous driver is cleared, unless secondarily triggered into an autonomous oscillatory state.

### Medical Condition–Coupled Bipolar Phenotype (formerly Bipolar

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A bipolar presentation arising from a demonstrable structural (CNS lesion), endocrine (Cushing’s, hyperthyroidism), or inflammatory (CNS lupus, multiple sclerosis) pathology. The manic/hypomanic/ depressed state is a direct physiological consequence of the medical condition rather than a primary limbic oscillator. The allostatic load is shared: the medical condition and the mood phenotype operate on the same pathophysiological substrate. Treatment of the underlying condition typically resolves the mood phenotype, barring permanent structural alteration.

### Subthreshold Bipolar Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Bipolar

**Allostatic Load Profile**

A heterogeneous collection of incomplete or truncated bipolar presentations that do not meet full duration or symptom thresholds. These represent the “boundary zone” of the oscillatory system: short-duration hypomania (2–3 days), hypomanic episodes with insufficient symptoms, hypomania without prior depression, or short-duration cyclothymia. The common feature is an oscillatory limbic drive that approximates but does not satisfy the time constants or amplitude thresholds of full phenotypes.

4. Right to conversion tracking: any subthreshold presentation that transitions to full-threshold criteria must be reclassified, not subsumed.
