# Module 08 — Dissociative Adaptations

Phenotypes from the Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6), reclassifying DSM-5 Dissociative Adaptations disorders through a neuro-somatic lens.

### Identity Fragmentation Phenotype (formerly Dissociative Identity Disorder 300.14 [F44.81])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Developmental trauma produces structural dissociation of consciousness via recurrent dorsolateral PFC suppression of limbic-encoded self-states during overwhelming events. Each personality state represents a distinct attractor basin in the neural network, with state-dependent access to autobiographical memory, affect regulation circuits, and somatic representation. Hippocampal memory integration fails across states due to dissociative amnesia mediated by prefrontal inhibition of hippocampal retrieval. Orbitofrontal cortex, hippocampus, parahippocampal gyrus, and amygdala structural alterations reported.

1. The Right to integrated hippocampal memory consolidation across all self-states

### Autobiographical Retrieval Suppression Phenotype (formerly Dissociative Amnesia 300.12 [F44.0] / With Dissociative Fugue 300.13 [F44.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Prefrontal cortex actively suppresses hippocampal retrieval of autobiographical information following overwhelming stress. Memory is successfully encoded (no structural hippocampal damage) but access is gated by prefrontal inhibitory circuits. In dissociative fugue, hippocampal spatial navigation circuits remain active while autobiographical identity circuits are suppressed, producing purposeful travel without self-awareness. Memory is always potentially reversible because storage is intact.

### Self-Unreality Phenotype (formerly Depersonalization/ Derealization Disorder 300.6 [F48.1])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Chronic prefrontal-limbic decoupling in which dorsolateral prefrontal cortex suppresses amygdala and insula interoceptive processing, producing subjective unreality of self and environment. Temporoparietal junction hyperactivation distorts self-location and body ownership. Physiological hyporeactivity to emotional stimuli reflects systemic suppression of limbic output. Neural substrates include HPA axis dysregulation, inferior parietal lobule, and prefrontal cortical-limbic circuits.

### Other Specified Dissociative Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 300.15 [F44.89])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Partial or atypical dissociative circuit dysregulation that does not meet threshold for full identity fragmentation, amnesia, or self-unreality phenotypes. Includes: chronic mixed dissociative symptoms without marked identity disruption; identity disturbance from coercive persuasion; acute (<1 month) dissociative reactions; dissociative trance.

### Unspecified Dissociative Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Dissociative Disorder 300.15 [F44.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Dissociative circuit dysregulation present but insufficient information for precise mapping.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The Right to provisional classification when data are insufficient for specific circuit mapping
2. The Right to re-evaluation when additional autonomic, pupillary, and neuroendocrine data become available.
