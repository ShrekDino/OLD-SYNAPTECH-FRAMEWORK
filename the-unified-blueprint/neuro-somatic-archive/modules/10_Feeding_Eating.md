# Module 10 — Feeding and Eating Adaptations

Phenotypes from the Forensic Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6), reclassifying DSM-5 Feeding and Eating Adaptations disorders through a neuro-somatic lens.

### Pica Phenotype (formerly Pica 307.52 [F98.3/F50.8])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Non-nutritive ingestive drive with dysregulated oral-sensory reward circuitry compelling consumption of nonfood substances. The organism’s mineral-sensing and gut-brain signaling pathways may signal deficiency, while the act of ingestion provides sensory reinforcement independent of nutritional value.

4. The right to distinction between culturally sanctioned practice and pathological ingestive drive.

### Rumination Phenotype (formerly Rumination Disorder 307.53 [F98.21])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Regurgitation-reflex dysregulation with habitual subdiaphragmatic vagal activation producing effortless retrograde food movement after ingestion. The organism engages a self-stimulating or self-soothing vagal circuit with repeated gastric-to-oral content transport independent of nausea or disgust.

1. The right to differentiation from structural GI disease via manometry and pH studies.

4. The right to recognition that regurgitation may serve a self-regulatory function requiring alternative vagal toning strategies.

### Anorexia Nervosa Phenotype (formerly Anorexia Nervosa 307.1 [F50.01/F50.02])

### Unspecified Feeding or Eating Phenotype (formerly Unspecified Feeding or Eating Disorder 307.50 [F50.9])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Uncharacterized feeding/eating circuit dysregulation with insufficient information for subtype specification.

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. The right to provisional classification without premature subtyping commitment.
2. The right to medical monitoring during diagnostic clarification.
3. The right to nutritional stabilization regardless of subtype classification.
4. The right to reclassification when sufficient neuro-somatic data are obtained.
