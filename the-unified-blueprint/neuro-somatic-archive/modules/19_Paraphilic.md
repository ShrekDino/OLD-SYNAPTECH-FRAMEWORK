# Module 19: Paraphilic Arousal Template Phenotypes

This module covers paraphilic phenotypes, reclassified from DSM-5 Paraphilic Disorders. Each phenotype is re-interpreted as an arousal template ontogeny — conditioned neurocircuitry linking specific stimuli to sexual reward, assessed through somatic proxy markers rather than moral judgment.

### Voyeuristic Phenotype (formerly Voyeuristic Disorder 302.82 [F65.3])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Arousal template ontogeny in which the reward circuitry is conditioned to non-consenting observation. Ventral striatum dopamine release during covert watching exceeds that during consenting dyadic interaction. Orbitofrontal cortex social-boundary detection impaired. Sympathetic hyperactivation during observation with reward-mediated reinforcement. Mirror neuron system underactivation during reciprocal sexual interaction.

### Pedophilic Phenotype (formerly Pedophilic Disorder 302.2 [F65.4])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Arousal template oriented to prepubescent rather than mature secondary sexual characteristics. Neurodevelopmental perturbation theory suggests in-utero or perinatal disruption of typical sexual maturation processing. Reduced amygdala response to adult sexual stimuli with enhanced ventral striatum response to prepubescent cues. Impaired temporal-lobe gating of sexual salience. Prefrontal inhibitory deficits increase acting-out risk when combined with antisociality.

### Other Specified Paraphilic Phenotype (formerly Other Specified Paraphilic Disorder 302.89 [F65.89])

**Allostatic Load Profile**

Arousal template conditioned to non-normophilic stimuli not covered by the eight listed phenotypes. Each variant involves specific sensory-limbic conditioning pathways: telephonic (auditory cortex → reward), necrophilic (somatosensory + threat-absence → reward), zoophilic (species-specific sensory → ventral striatum), coprophilic/urophilic (olfactory-gustatory → limbic), klismaphilic (viscerosensory → reward).

**Biological Bill of Rights**

1. Right to individualized neuro-somatic assessment for each specific arousal template
2. Right to treatment focused on the specific sensory-limbic pathway involved
3. Right to freedom from pathologization when no distress, impairment, or victimization
4. Right to harm-reduction approaches for high-risk variants (necrophilia, zoophilia)
