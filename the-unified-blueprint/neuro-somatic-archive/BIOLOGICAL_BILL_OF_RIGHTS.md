# Biological Bill of Rights

## Preamble

Every phenotype cataloged in the Neuro-Somatic Archive is a description of a biological state — not a diagnosis, not a label, not a moral judgment. The human organism adapts to the environments it is given. When those environments are toxic, extractive, neglectful, or violent, the organism responds in the only language it has: the language of neuroendocrine signaling, autonomic tone, synaptic architecture, and inflammatory cascades. These responses are not disorders. They are biological facts.

The following rights attach to every individual who enters the Archive's classification system. They are not aspirational principles. They are **operational constraints** on how assessment, classification, and intervention may proceed. No phenotype may be assigned, no treatment administered, no legal determination made, in violation of these rights. They are the constitutional layer of the forensic framework.

---

### 1. Right to Biological Assessment

No phenotype shall be assigned without objective biological measurement. Self-report alone is insufficient. Every classification must be grounded in at least one forensic proxy — a manually administrable, equipment-free test producing a quantifiable biological marker. Where proxies are unavailable or contraindicated, classification defaults to "Unspecified — Insufficient Data" rather than to a presumptive DSM-5 equivalent.

### 2. Right to Allostatic Interpretation

Every "symptom" shall be interpreted as an adaptive biological response to environmental demands, not as a pathological malfunction. The burden of proof falls on anyone claiming a trait is disordered rather than adaptive. The default assumption — the null hypothesis of the Archive — is that the organism is responding appropriately to its environment. Pathology is the exception, not the rule.

### 3. Right to Entropy Threshold Classification

No individual shall receive a binary yes/no diagnosis. Classification shall occur exclusively within the three-state Entropy Threshold system: **Protected Phenotype**, **Transition Zone**, or **Somatic Debt**. These states describe the thermodynamic integrity of the system, not the presence or absence of a "disorder." No threshold is permanent; all are subject to reassessment.

### 4. Right to Zero-Cost Verification

All proxy assessments required for classification shall be manually administrable with no equipment cost beyond a timing device and a penlight. No individual shall be required to undergo expensive laboratory testing, imaging, or proprietary assessment tools to establish their biological state. The Archive is designed for prisons, refugee camps, occupied territories, rural clinics, and homeless shelters — not only university hospitals.

### 5. Right to Phenotypic Advantage Analysis

Every trait identified as a phenotype shall also be analyzed for potential adaptive advantage in specific environments. This analysis is not a rhetorical exercise. It is a required field in the Somatic Architecture Table. The question is not "Is this trait disordered?" but "Under what conditions would this trait be an advantage?" — and those conditions shall be documented.

### 6. Right to Data Sufficiency

No permanent classification shall be made without adequate longitudinal data. A single assessment point is insufficient to determine entropy threshold classification. The Archive requires multiple proxy measurements across time, preferably across varying environmental conditions, before a classification may be considered established. Until then, the status is provisional.

### 7. Right to Provisional Status

"Unspecified" is not a diagnosis. It is a statement of insufficient data. No individual shall be treated as though they have a condition solely because the Archive lacks complete information about their biological state. Unspecified status carries no presumption of pathology. It carries only an acknowledgment that the assessment is incomplete.

### 8. Right to Intervention Proportionality

Interventions — whether pharmacological, environmental, social, or somatic — shall be proportional to demonstrated allostatic severity. Full syndromal criteria shall not be required to access treatment. If proxy measurements indicate a system under stress, intervention is warranted regardless of whether the phenotype meets DSM-5 thresholds. Conversely, meeting DSM-5 criteria without demonstrated allostatic load does not automatically warrant intervention.

### 9. Right to Longitudinal Reassessment

Allostatic states evolve. Classification must evolve with them. Every individual shall have the right to periodic reassessment, and any classification may be changed when new data warrants it. No classification is permanent. The Archive does not believe in fixed diagnoses — it believes in biological trajectories.

### 10. Right to Freedom from Moral Judgment

Biological states are not moral failures. No phenotype shall be described using language that implies volitional failure, character defect, or moral weakness. The Archive prohibits terms such as "disorder," "defect," "dysfunction," "impairment," "abnormal," and "pathological" as classification labels. These terms may appear in historical cross-references to the DSM-5 but shall not be used as primary descriptors. The organism does what it must to survive. The Archive documents what it does. It does not judge it.

---

## Enforcement

These rights are structurally embedded in the Archive's data model. Every Somatic Architecture Table includes a rights check field. Every phenotype assignment is invalid if it violates any of these rights. Every proxy protocol includes a confirmation step that the administrator has verified compliance.

The Biological Bill of Rights is not a separate document appended to the Archive. It is the Archive's operating system. Classification without these rights is not classification — it is re-pathologization under a new name. The Archive refuses this outcome by design.
