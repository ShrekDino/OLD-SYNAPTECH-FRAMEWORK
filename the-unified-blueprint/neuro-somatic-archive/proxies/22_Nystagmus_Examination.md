# 22. Nystagmus Examination (for Substance Intoxication / Neurocognitive)

**Physiological Substrate:** Vestibular-ocular reflex — sensitive to cerebellar suppression from alcohol, sedatives, and anticonvulsants.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Horizontal gaze: patient follows examiner's finger to 45° lateral, hold
2. Observe for sustained nystagmus — note direction and amplitude
3. Vertical gaze: follow finger up 45°, observe
4. End-point nystagmus: normal at extreme deviation; distinguish from sustained nystagmus

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Sustained horizontal nystagmus <45° | Alcohol/sedative intoxication or cerebellar pathology |
| Vertical nystagmus | Brainstem pathology — not intoxication alone |
| Positional nystagmus | Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo vs. central |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Alcohol Intoxication/Withdrawal, Sedative/Hypnotic Intoxication, Cerebellar Neurocognitive, Wernicke Encephalopathy, Anticonvulsant Toxicity.
