# 28. Centrifugal Sweat Test (Quantitative Sudomotor)

**Physiological Substrate:** Postganglionic sympathetic cholinergic sudomotor fibers — one of the first to degenerate in peripheral autonomic neuropathies.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Apply starch-iodine or a dry thermal paper to dorsum of foot
2. Induce sweating via: (a) 1 min of moderate exercise (step-ups), (b) gustatory (capsaicin)
3. Observe sweat droplet pattern after 60s

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Uniform sweat droplets | Normal |
| Patchy or absent | Peripheral autonomic neuropathy |
| Global anhidrosis | Severe autonomic failure |
| Hyperhidrosis | Sympathetic excess (anxiety, hyperthyroid, pheochromocytoma) |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Peripheral Neuropathy (diabetic, alcoholic), Somatic Symptom Disorder, Dysautonomia, Panic Disorder, Hyperthyroidism, Pheochromocytoma.
