# 2. Pupillary Light Reflex (PLR) — Constriction Velocity

**Physiological Substrate:** Parasympathetic sphincter pupillae (CN III) vs. sympathetic dilator pupillae. Constriction velocity reflects parasympathetic integrity; baseline diameter reflects sympathetic tone.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Dim ambient light to standard low level
2. Allow 30 seconds adaptation
3. Hold penlight 15 cm lateral to eye
4. Sweep light medially across pupil in 0.5 seconds
5. Measure: (a) baseline diameter, (b) constriction velocity (mm/s via ruler held at brow), (c) latency to constriction onset
6. Repeat 3 times per eye, average

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Measure | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline diameter (dark) | 4–6 mm | >6.5 mm = sympathetic hypertonus; <3 mm = parasympathetic excess |
| Constriction velocity | >3.5 mm/s | <3.0 mm/s = midbrain integration deficit |
| Constriction latency | <0.35 s | >0.45 s = tectal processing delay |
| Dilation recovery | >1.5 mm/s | <1.0 mm/s = sympathetic exhaustion |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Autism Spectrum, Schizophrenia, Delusional Disorder, Bipolar (manic phase), PTSD, Panic Disorder, Generalized Anxiety, Substance Intoxication/Withdrawal, Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome, Antidepressant Discontinuation, Neurocognitive Disorders, Parkinsonism.
