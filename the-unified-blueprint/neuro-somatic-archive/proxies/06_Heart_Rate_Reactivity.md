# 6. Heart Rate Reactivity (Orthostatic Challenge or Script-Driven)

**Physiological Substrate:** Baroreflex-mediated autonomic response to postural change or emotional provocation.

**Administration Protocol:**
6A. Orthostatic HR Challenge

1. Measure HR supine (1 min rest) — count radial pulse × 4 = HR
2. Patient stands upright (active stand, 10s)
3. Measure HR at 30s and 120s standing

6B. Script-Driven HR Reactivity

1. Patient reads or hears personalized stressor script for 30s
2. Measure HR before, during, and 60s after
3. Compute: peak HR − baseline HR; recovery time to baseline

**Pathological Thresholds:**
**6A. Orthostatic HR Challenge**

| Response | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| ΔHR at 30s | 10–20 bpm increase | >30 bpm = autonomic dysregulation; <5 bpm = autonomic failure |
| HR at 120s | Within 10 bpm of supine | Persistent elevation >15 bpm = sympathetic excess |

**6B. Script-Driven HR Reactivity**

| Measure | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| Peak ΔHR | <15 bpm | >30 bpm = conditioned autonomic response |
| Recovery time | <60s | >120s = delayed vagal re-engagement |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** PTSD, Panic Disorder, Specific Phobia, Social Anxiety, Generalized Anxiety, Borderline Personality, Somatic Symptom Disorder, Conversion Disorder, Orthostatic Intolerance, POTS.
