# 11. Gait Speed (5-Meter Walk)

**Physiological Substrate:** Integrated output of motor cortex, basal ganglia, cerebellum, proprioceptive afferents — sensitive to global neurological integrity.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Mark 5 m course
2. Patient walks at comfortable pace
3. Time from first foot movement over start line to first foot past end line
4. Compute: gait speed = 5 m / time (s)
5. Repeat with dual-task (walk while subtracting serial 3s)

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Gait Speed | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| >1.2 m/s | Normal |
| 0.8–1.2 m/s | Mild slowing — early neurocognitive decline |
| 0.6–0.8 m/s | Moderate — increased fall risk |
| <0.6 m/s | Severe — functional dependence |
| Dual-task decrement >20% | Cognitive-motor interference — frontal executive deficit |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Neurocognitive Disorders (all subtypes), Parkinson's Disease, Huntington's, Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus, Cerebellar Degeneration, Major Depressive (psychomotor retardation), Catatonia, Antipsychotic-Induced Parkinsonism.
