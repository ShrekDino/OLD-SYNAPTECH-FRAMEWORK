# 9. Deep Tendon Reflex (DTR) — Patellar

**Physiological Substrate:** Spinal monosynaptic stretch reflex — modulated by supraspinal descending pathways (corticospinal inhibitory, reticulospinal facilitatory).

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Patient seated, knees flexed 90°, feet unsupported
2. Strike patellar tendon with reflex hammer (or side of firm hand)
3. Grade using 0–4 scale
4. Test with Jendrassik maneuver (hook fingers and pull apart) to enhance

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Grade | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0 | Areflexia — peripheral neuropathy, hypothyroidism, severe cerebellar disease |
| 1+ | Hyporeflexia — reduced descending facilitatory drive (psychomotor retardation, catatonia) |
| 2+ | Normal |
| 3+ | Hyperreflexia — upper motor neuron, serotonin syndrome, stimulant excess |
| 4+ | Clonus — neuroleptic malignant syndrome, severe hypertonia |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Major Depressive (hyporeflexia = psychomotor retardation), Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (hyperreflexia + clonus), Serotonin Syndrome, Catatonia, Parkinson's, Neurocognitive Disorders, Bipolar (manic = hyperreflexia), Tardive Dyskinesia, Stimulant Intoxication.
