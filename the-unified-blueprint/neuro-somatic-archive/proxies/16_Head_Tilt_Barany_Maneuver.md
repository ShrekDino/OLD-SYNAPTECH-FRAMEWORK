# 16. Head Tilt / Barany Maneuver (for Conversion/FND)

**Physiological Substrate:** Vestibular-ocular reflex and cerebellar integration — a positive test in conversion is dissociation of subjective dizziness from objective nystagmus.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Patient seated, observe for nystagmus at rest
2. Head impulse test: rapid head turn 20°, observe catch-up saccade
3. Dix-Hallpike: patient supine, head turned 45°, extend neck 20°, observe for nystagmus + vertigo

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Dissociation between reported vertigo (severe) and observed nystagmus (absent/normal) | Functional (conversion) phenotype |
| Objective nystagmus with matching vertigo | Vestibular pathology |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Conversion Disorder (Functional Neurological Symptom), Somatic Symptom Disorder.
