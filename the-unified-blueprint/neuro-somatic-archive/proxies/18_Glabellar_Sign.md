# 18. Glabellar Sign (Myerson's Sign)

**Physiological Substrate:** Frontal release sign — failure of inhibitory control over brainstem blink reflex due to frontal-subcortical degeneration.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Standing in front of patient
2. Tap glabella (between eyebrows) firmly with index finger at 1 Hz
3. Observe blink response — normally extinguishes after 2–3 taps
4. Continue for 10 taps

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Response | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Extinguishes by tap 3–4 | Normal |
| Persistent blinking through tap 10 | Positive Myerson's = frontal release / basal ganglia pathology |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Parkinson's Disease, Progressive Supranuclear Palsy, Neurocognitive Disorders with frontal involvement, Lewy Body Disease, Major Depressive (severe psychomotor slowing).
