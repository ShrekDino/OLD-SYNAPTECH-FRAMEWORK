# 21. Tinetti Balance Assessment (Modified)

**Physiological Substrate:** Vestibular, proprioceptive, cerebellar, and basal ganglia integration for postural stability.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Sitting balance: observe for sway
2. Standing balance: feet together, eyes open (30s)
3. Standing balance: feet together, eyes closed (30s) — Romberg
4. Pull test: examiner pulls shoulders backward from behind, observe recovery
5. Timed up-and-go: stand, walk 3 m, turn, return, sit

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Test | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| Romberg (EC) | Minimal sway, >30s | Sway increase >50% = proprioceptive/vibration deficit |
| Pull test | 0–1 step recovery | >2 steps or fall = postural instability |
| Timed up-and-go | <12s | >15s = fall risk |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Parkinson's Disease, Huntington's, Cerebellar Degeneration, Neurocognitive Disorders, Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus, Antipsychotic-Induced Parkinsonism, Alcohol Intoxication/Withdrawal.
