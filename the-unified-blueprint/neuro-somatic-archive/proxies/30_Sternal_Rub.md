# 30. Sternal Rub / Pain Response Assessment

**Physiological Substrate:** Spinothalamic tract → thalamus → anterior cingulate for pain affect. Used to assess level of consciousness and dissociative vs. neurological motor deficit.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Apply knuckle to sternum with firm pressure
2. Observe: facial expression, limb withdrawal, vocalization, eye opening
3. Grade: 0 = no response; 1 = grimace; 2 = localization; 3 = withdrawal; 4 = purposeful avoidance

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| No response | Coma, stupor, catatonia, dissociative state (with normal EEG) |
| Withdrawal without grimace | Conversion (dissociation of motor and affective components) |
| Appropriate grimace + withdrawal | Normal pain processing |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Catatonia, Conversion Disorder (Functional Neurological Symptom — dissociative sensory loss), Dissociative Disorders, Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome, Delirium, Unspecified Catatonia.
