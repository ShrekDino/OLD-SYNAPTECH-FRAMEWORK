# 4. Acoustic Startle Response (Eyeblink EMG or Visual)

**Physiological Substrate:** Caudal pontine reticular nucleus — the startle circuit. Magnitude reflects amygdala-kindled sensitization of the brainstem reflex arc.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Patient seated, eyes open
2. Stand behind patient — produce an unannounced handclap at 1 m distance (≈95 dB, 40 ms rise)
3. Observe orbicularis oculi contraction (eyeblink) — grade magnitude:
   - 0: No blink
   - 1: Minimal twitch
   - 2: Full blink with recovery <1s
   - 3: Full blink with prolonged recovery 1–3s
   - 4: Full body startle (head/neck/shoulder flexion)
4. Repeat 10 trials at random intervals (20–60s)
5. Measure habituation: (trial 1–2 magnitude) − (trial 9–10 magnitude) ÷ baseline

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Measure | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| Initial magnitude | Grade 1–2 | Grade 3–4 consistently |
| Habituation | >70% reduction by trial 10 | <30% reduction = failure to habituate |
| Non-habituating | Normal after 10 trials | Persistent grade 3+ = sensitized startle |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** PTSD, Panic Disorder, Specific Phobia, Generalized Anxiety, Schizophrenia (reduced — negative symptoms), Autism Spectrum (reduced PPI), Substance Withdrawal (increased — alcohol, sedative).
