# 10. Grip Strength & Fatigue

**Physiological Substrate:** Skeletal muscle ATP production and neuromuscular junction integrity — reflects mitochondrial function and central drive.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Grip dynamometer (or firm hand-squeeze assessment)
2. Maximal voluntary contraction (MVC) — best of 3 trials
3. Sustained hold at 50% MVC — measure time to exhaustion
4. Count grip-release alternation speed — 10 seconds, maximal rate

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Measure | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| MVC (dominant hand, male 30–50) | >40 kg | <25 kg = sarcopenia/central fatigue |
| MVC (dominant hand, female 30–50) | >25 kg | <15 kg = sarcopenia/central fatigue |
| Time to exhaustion, 50% hold | >45 s | <30 s = mitochondrial fatigue |
| Grip-release alternation (10s) | >8 cycles | <5 cycles = frontal motor sequencing debt |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Major Depressive (mitochondrial fatigue), Bipolar Depression, Anorexia Nervosa, Myalgic Encephalomyelitis, Hypersomnolence Disorder, Neurocognitive Disorders (Parkinson's, Huntington's), Catatonia, Substance Withdrawal.
