# 7. Skin Conductance Response / Palmar Sweat (Visual)

**Physiological Substrate:** Eccrine sweat glands — exclusively sympathetic (cholinergic) innervation. Visible sweat onset correlates with >2 µS increase.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Observe palmar surface of non-dominant hand at rest (30s)
2. Deliver cognitive load (serial 7 subtraction) or emotional probe
3. Grade visible sweat onset: 0 = dry; 1 = minimal moisture; 2 = visible bead formation; 3 = frank sweating/drops
4. Measure latency from stimulus to visible sweat

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Measure | Normal | Pathological |
|---|---|---|
| Resting | Dry (Grade 0) | Grade 1+ = tonic sympathetic activation |
| Latency to sweat | >15s to cognitive load | <5s = kindled SCR |
| Recovery to dry | <30s | >120s = delayed sympathetic shutoff |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** PTSD, Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety, Generalized Anxiety, Specific Phobia, Bipolar (manic), Stimulant Intoxication, Alcohol Withdrawal, Antisocial Personality (reduced).
