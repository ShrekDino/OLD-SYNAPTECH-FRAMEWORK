# 27. Dexamethasone Suppression Test (DST)

**Physiological Substrate:** HPA axis negative feedback — dexamethasone (synthetic glucocorticoid) suppresses ACTH → cortisol production in healthy feedback; failure indicates glucocorticoid receptor resistance.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Administer 1 mg dexamethasone PO at 23:00
2. Draw serum cortisol at 08:00 and 16:00 next day
3. (Alternative: salivary cortisol at same timepoints)

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Post-DST Cortisol | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| <1.8 µg/dL (50 nmol/L) | Normal suppression |
| 1.8–5 µg/dL | Partial escape — early HPA dysregulation |
| >5 µg/dL | Escape — GR resistance (major depressive, PTSD, chronic stress) |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Major Depressive (melancholic — highest non-suppression rate), PTSD (often low cortisol + enhanced suppression), Bipolar Depression, Anorexia Nervosa, Chronic Insomnia.
