# 1. Vagal Tone via Respiratory Sinus Arrhythmia (RSA)

**Physiological Substrate:** Myelinated vagus nerve (ventral vagal complex, nucleus ambiguus) modulates heart rate with respiration — accelerating on inspiration, decelerating on expiration.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Seat patient upright, feet flat, hands visible
2. Instruct: "Breathe in for 4 seconds, out for 6 seconds" — demonstrate
3. Palpate radial pulse at wrist — locate maximal impulse with index and middle finger
4. Count pulse during 3 consecutive breath cycles
5. Record beats during peak inspiration and end-expiration
6. Compute: Δ RSA = HR inspiration − HR expiration

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Δ RSA | Classification | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| >14 bpm | Robust vagal tone | Intact ventral vagal brake |
| 10–14 bpm | Adequate | Normal range |
| 8–10 bpm | Borderline | Early vagal withdrawal |
| 5–8 bpm | Reduced | Moderate vagal compromise |
| <5 bpm | Critical | Severe vagal brake failure |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Autism Spectrum, PTSD, Generalized Anxiety, Panic, Social Anxiety, Major Depressive, Borderline Personality, Somatic Symptom, Dissociative Identity, Conversion Disorder, Functional Neurological, Feeding Restriction, Insomnia, Substance Withdrawal (all classes), Paraphilic Arousal — every phenotype with autonomic dysregulation.
