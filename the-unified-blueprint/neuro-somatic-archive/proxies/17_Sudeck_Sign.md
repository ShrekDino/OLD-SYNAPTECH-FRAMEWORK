# 17. Sudeck's Sign / Skin Temperature Asymmetry

**Physiological Substrate:** Sympathetic vasomotor tone — reduced skin temperature indicates vasoconstriction from local or reflex sympathetic hypertonus.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Palpate bilateral dorsal hands with dorsum of examiner's hand
2. Grade temperature difference: 0 = no difference; 1 = subtle; 2 = clearly colder; 3 = frank coldness
3. Note: color changes (pallor, cyanosis, mottling)
4. Confirm with observation of nail bed capillary refill

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Asymmetry ≥ grade 2 | Complex regional pain syndrome / conversion / sympathetic maintained pain |
| Bilateral cold | Systemic sympathetic excess |
| Bilateral warm with flushing | Vasodilation — alcohol, histamine, mast cell activation |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Conversion Disorder, Somatic Symptom Disorder (with pain), Complex Regional Pain Syndrome, Factitious Disorder, Panic Disorder (peripheral vasoconstriction).
