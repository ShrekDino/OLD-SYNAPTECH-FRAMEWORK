# 20. Interoceptive Accuracy (Heartbeat Counting Task)

**Physiological Substrate:** Anterior insula — interoceptive prediction error detection. Reduced accuracy reflects insular noise or dissociation.

**Administration Protocol:**
1. Patient seated, quiet
2. Without feeling pulse or chest, patient counts their own heartbeats for three intervals: 25s, 35s, 45s (random order)
3. Simultaneously, examiner counts actual heartbeats via stethoscope or pulse
4. Compute: Accuracy score = 1 − (|actual − counted| / actual) averaged across intervals

**Pathological Thresholds:**
| Accuracy Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| >0.85 | High interoceptive sensitivity |
| 0.70–0.85 | Adequate |
| 0.50–0.70 | Reduced interoceptive accuracy |
| <0.50 | Significant interoceptive noise / dissociation |

**Phenotype Cross-Reference:** Panic Disorder (elevated accuracy), Autism Spectrum (reduced), Anorexia Nervosa (reduced), Alexithymia, PTSD (variable), Somatic Symptom, Hypochondriasis, Conversion Disorder, Dissociative Disorders (reduced).
