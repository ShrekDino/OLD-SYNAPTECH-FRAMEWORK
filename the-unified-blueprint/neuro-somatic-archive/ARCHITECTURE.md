# Neuro-Somatic Architecture — Forensic DSM-6 Framework

## The Allostatic Load Model

Allostatic load is the cumulative biological cost of adaptation to environmental demands. Every organism maintains viability through continuous adjustment of its internal milieu in response to external pressures. When these adjustments become chronic, the system accumulates debt — not in ledgers, but in cortisol curves, inflammatory markers, autonomic tone, and synaptic pruning patterns. This is not pathology. This is physics.

The DSM-5 calls these debts "disorders." The Neuro-Somatic Archive calls them what they are: **measurable states of biological investment**. A panic surge is not a malfunction; it is a threat-response system operating at full gain because the environment demanded it. Depression is not a chemical imbalance; it is an energy-conservation strategy deployed when expected returns on metabolic expenditure no longer materialize. The Archive does not pathologize adaptation. It quantifies it.

### Allostasis vs. Homeostasis

Homeostasis presumes a single optimal set point — a biological thermostat set to 37°C, a pH held at 7.4. It is a model of constancy. Allostasis recognizes that the set point itself changes with context. Stability is achieved *through* change, not despite it. A febrile body raises its temperature to fight infection; the set point shifts because the environment demands it. The same logic applies to mood, arousal, attention, and social behavior. The Archive tracks these shifting set points and measures the metabolic cost of maintaining them. Homeostasis asks: "Is this within normal range?" Allostasis asks: "What did it cost to keep this system stable, and is that cost sustainable?"

## The Entropy Threshold Classification

The Archive replaces categorical yes/no diagnosis with a three-state thermodynamic model of system integrity. Every phenotype is classified at one of three thresholds based on allostatic marker readings, proxy-verified biological evidence, and longitudinal trajectory.

### Protected Phenotype

**Biological markers within adaptive range.** The system is responding to environmental demands without accumulating unsustainable debt. Stress recovery curves normalize within expected timeframes. Inflammatory markers, cortisol awakening response, heart rate variability, and interoceptive accuracy all fall within ranges compatible with long-term system integrity. The individual may experience distress, but the biological substrate is not degrading. **Intervention:** Monitoring, environmental modification, phenotypic advantage analysis. No forced labeling.

### Transition Zone

**Elevated reactivity with partial system engagement.** Some markers cross adaptive thresholds. Stress recovery is delayed but not absent. Intermittent dysregulation appears — episodic hyperarousal, blunted affect, sleep fragmentation, executive function degradation under load. The system is paying a price, but it has not collapsed. This zone is where most DSM-5 "diagnoses" would fall, but the Archive refuses the binary. **Intervention:** Proportional — allostatic load reduction, targeted proxy monitoring, environmental restructuring. Treatment does not require full syndromal criteria; it requires demonstrated system stress.

### Somatic Debt

**Full system failure with life-threatening risk.** Biological markers in pathological ranges. The allostatic debt is compounding faster than the organism can repay. Inflammatory cascades become self-sustaining. Autonomic regulation collapses into fixed modes — either hypervigilant freeze or dissociative shutdown. Sleep architecture disintegrates. Metabolic dysregulation reaches organ-system involvement. This is not "severe mental illness"; it is **somatic system failure** requiring immediate structural intervention. **Intervention:** Maximum intensity — medical stabilization, environmental extraction, forensic documentation, legal protection.

## The Forensic Proxy Protocol

The DSM-5 depends on subjective self-report — the patient's capacity to articulate internal states. This is epistemically fragile. The Archive replaces subjective reporting with **zero-cost forensic proxies**: 30 manual clinical assessments requiring no equipment beyond a timing device and a penlight. Each proxy is a biological measurement that can be administered in any setting — prison cell, homeless shelter, war zone, rural clinic.

Proxies include: pupillary light reflex latency, orthostatic heart rate change, cold pressor recovery time, breath-hold duration, saccadic velocity estimation, grip-and-release fatigue rate, startle recovery timing, tympanic temperature asymmetry, and 22 others. Each cross-references to specific phenotypes and entropy thresholds. The proxy protocol ensures that classification is **forensic** — grounded in observable, reproducible, equipment-free biological measurement — rather than confessional.

## The Phenotypic Advantage Analysis

Every trait cataloged in this Archive carries a **dual-aspect valuation**. What the DSM-5 codes as a "symptom" may be, in a different environment, a functional advantage. Attention dysregulation (ADHD) confers rapid environmental scanning in high-risk contexts. Social-pragmatic divergence (autism) enables pattern recognition at the expense of social fluency. Anxiety spectrum hypervigilance is threat-detection operating at maximum sensitivity — and it kept our ancestors alive.

The Archive does not romanticize suffering. But it refuses to pathologize traits that are only maladaptive within specific economic or social structures. The **Phenotypic Advantage Analysis** is not a consolation prize. It is a biological reality check: if the environment were different, would this still be a "disorder"?

## Systemic Hardening

A system that has survived chronic allostatic load is not weaker. It is **hardened** — like bone remodeling under repeated stress, like synaptic pruning that optimizes for the environment that actually exists rather than the one the textbooks describe. The Archive recognizes that many "disordered" phenotypes represent systemic hardening: adaptations that are costly but survivable. The goal is not to return the system to a "baseline" that never existed. The goal is to reduce allostatic load to the point where the hardened system can operate without accumulating further debt.

You do not cure bone. You stop the repeated fracture.
